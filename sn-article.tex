\abstract{
We formulate Universal Coset Field Theory (UCFT) as an axiomatic four-dimensional
operator-algebraic quantum field theory whose local observable net is
conditionally rigidly determined by the Albert algebra $\Albert$ and the compact
EIII geometry of type $\ESix$. The theory is represented by an
$\ESix$-invariant coset sector with vacuum orbit
\(\ESix/(\SOTenXUOne)\). The associated Haag--Kastler net contains local
coset, gauge, matter, and spectral subalgebras, while the Euclidean functional
integral is used only as a generator of Schwinger functions for this
operator-algebraic theory. Spontaneous symmetry breaking produces \(32\)
Goldstone directions forming an irreducible \(\SOTenXUOne\) multiplet with
complexification
\(\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}\) and a unique positive
invariant metric. A spacetime-filling coset representation induces a
four-dimensional Riemannian metric by pull-back, and the Einstein--Hilbert term
appears as a one-loop effective term in the Sakharov--Adler sense. Within the
renormalization-group truncation studied, the positive-coupling flow is
effective only below the boundary \(\epsilon<1/6\); a heterotic ultraviolet
completion is therefore formulated as a matching condition, not as an
autonomous coset fixed point. The zeta sector is a commutative spectral
subalgebra of the coset observable algebra: a positive Galois-equivariant
realization of the shifted zeta divisor in this subalgebra implies the Riemann
hypothesis. We conclude UCFT is an algebraic quantum field theory in which
geometry, gravity, and arithmetic spectral constraints are emergent from the
coset representation of the local observable algebra.
}

\section{Introduction}
%======================================================================%



Exceptional algebraic structures recur throughout high-energy physics. The
exceptional Jordan (Albert) algebra $\Albert$ of $3\times3$ Hermitian octonionic
matrices has automorphism group the compact $F_{4}$, and its norm-preserving
reduced structure group is the noncompact real form $\E_{6(-26)}$ of the
complex Lie type $\ESix$, the type that preserves its cubic norm
\cite{Jacobson:1968,Springer:2000,McCrimmon:2004}. All coset constructions in
this paper use the compact real form of the same complex
type\footnote{Throughout, $\ESix$ denotes the compact real form of the complex
Lie type $\E_6$. It acts on the complexified Albert algebra $\AlbertC$ (the
minimal $\mathbf{27}_{\mathbb C}$) and is the group entering the EIII coset
construction; it does not act on the real slice $\Albert$. The noncompact form
$\E_{6(-26)}$ appears only as the reduced structure group of the real Albert
algebra. See \autoref{rmk:RealForms}.}, acting on the complexified Albert
algebra $\AlbertC$.
It contains $\SOTenXUOne$, under which the fundamental
$\mathbf{27}$ branches as
\[
  \mathbf{27}=\mathbf{16}_{+1}\oplus\mathbf{10}_{-2}\oplus\mathbf{1}_{+4},
\]
the $\mathbf{16}$ of $\Spin(10)$ carrying one Standard-Model family together
with a right-handed neutrino \cite{Slansky:1981}. Formally real Jordan algebras
were introduced as algebras of quantum observables; $\Albert$ is the unique
exceptional member of their classification. The organizing question is how much
four-dimensional field theory is fixed once this exceptional structure and its
compact $\ESix$ symmetry are specified.

The main result of this paper is a conditional rigidity statement in the
language of algebraic quantum field theory. The primary object of UCFT is a
net \(\mathcal O\mapsto\mathfrak A(\mathcal O)\) of local observable
\(C^{*}\)-algebras on the reconstructed four-dimensional spacetime. Once the
Albert structure, the compact EIII coset geometry, and the quantum
kinematical postulate are fixed, the coset representation of this net has no
independent gravitational input. No graviton field, no spacetime metric, and no
gravitational action are postulated in that sector. A spacetime-filling
representation of the EIII coset induces its metric by pull-back
(\autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}), and the Einstein--Hilbert term appears
radiatively, at one loop, in the Sakharov--Adler sense
(\autoref{thm:inducedEH}).

The construction rests on two postulates. The first postulate posits a maximally
symmetric Albert structure; the second posits the usual quantum-mechanical
kinematics of a Hilbert-space representation with unitary dynamics. The local
observable net is then represented by an $\ESix$-invariant symmetry-breaking
sector on the compact EIII coset. A polynomial potential on $\AlbertC$,
built from the invariant Hermitian form and the Freudenthal adjugate, is
coercive and is minimized on the orbit of unit-norm rank-one elements; the
corresponding orbit of complex rays is
\[
  \ESix\cdot [X_{0}]\cong\MCoset,
\]
the compact Hermitian symmetric space EIII \cite{Helgason:1978}, of real
dimension $78-46=32$. After the gauged $\UOne_\psi\subset H$ absorbs the
overall phase mode (\autoref{cor:VacuumManifold}), the $32$ physical Goldstone
directions form a single irreducible
multiplet of $H=\SOTenXUOne$, whose complexification is
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$; the invariant metric is
therefore unique up to scale and positive definite.

The coset representation also supplies the geometric data. A
spacetime-filling configuration of the Goldstone fields immerses a
four-manifold into EIII; its first fundamental form is a nondegenerate
Riemannian metric, so the four-dimensional geometry is induced by the
representation rather than postulated independently. The Euclidean Schwinger
functions of the observable net are reconstructed by the
Osterwalder--Schrader theorem. The one-loop effective action of the coset,
gauge, and chiral \(\mathbf{16}\) sectors induces an Einstein--Hilbert term in
the sense of Sakharov \cite{SakharovInducedGravity,AdlerInducedGravity}; the
metric enters through this induced action rather than through a fundamental
graviton.

Two additional sectoral rigidity tests are analyzed. First, within the stated
functional renormalization-group truncation, the coset-field action is an
effective description below the cutoff \(\epsilon<1/6\). The positive-coupling
flow does not supply an interacting asymptotic-safety fixed point; therefore an
$\E_{8}\times \E_{8}$ heterotic ultraviolet completion is formulated as a
matching criterion at the boundary. The corresponding heterotic matching scale
is \(M_{\mathrm{GUT}}\simeq2\times10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}\), while plain
non-supersymmetric Standard-Model one-loop running would cross near
\(10^{13}\,\mathrm{GeV}\). Second, the $\ESix$ branchings supply a
grand-unified matter skeleton and anomaly constraints. We also explain how the
Riemann zeta function enters as a conditional spectral rigidity criterion for a
commutative spectral subalgebra of the coset observable algebra: if the shifted
zeta divisor admits a positive Galois-equivariant realization in that
subalgebra, then the Riemann hypothesis follows.

The paper is organized as follows. \autoref{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB} states the
postulates, formulates the operator-algebraic QFT, recalls the Albert algebra and

\autoref{sec:EmergentFields} derives the emergent four-dimensional geometry and
the low-energy field content represented in the local net. \autoref{sec:sigma}
defines the coset $\sigma$-model representation, computes its Euclidean
Schwinger functions, and derives the induced Einstein--Hilbert term and the
coset mass spectrum. \autoref{sec:FRG} presents the renormalization-group flow,
its \(\epsilon<1/6\) validity window, and the grand-unified matching scale.
\autoref{sec:ZetaSector} explains the zeta function through the coset
correspondence and gives the spectral reformulation of the Riemann hypothesis.
\autoref{sec:StringTheory} states the conditional heterotic matching criterion,
records the $\mathbb Z_{12}$ orbit test, and formulates the resulting relative
Albert--EIII swampland criterion.
\autoref{sec:Discussion} separates the unconditional representation-theoretic
results from the conditional ones and makes the logical dependencies among
them explicit. \autoref{sec:Conclusion} closes with a summary of what has been established.
Proofs and technical computations are collected in the appendices.

\section{Postulates, the Albert Algebra, and Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking}
\label{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB}
%======================================================================%
Here, we state the postulates, formulate the local observable net, recall
the Albert algebra and the compact $\ESix$ symmetry used in the coset
representation, construct the algebraic symmetry-breaking potential, and
count the resulting $32$ Goldstone directions.

\begin{postulate}[Maximally Symmetric Vacuum]\label{ax:PostulateI}
The vacuum order parameter takes values in the complexified Albert algebra
$\AlbertC$, and its dynamics are invariant under the full compact exceptional
symmetry selected by the Albert algebra: the compact real form $\ESix$ acting
on $\AlbertC$.
\end{postulate}
\begin{postulate}[Universal Hilbert-Space Dynamics]\label{ax:PostulateII}
Physical states $\psi\in\mathcal H$ evolve through a one-parameter family
$\{U(t)\}_{t\in\mathbb R}$ of unitary operators satisfying
$U(0)=\mathrm{Id}$, $U(t+s)=U(t)U(s)$, and strong continuity.
\end{postulate}

\begin{remark}
\autoref{ax:PostulateII} provides the kinematical Hilbert space and the
one-parameter family of unitary operators. The local observable algebra is
specified below. Whenever we later write a
functional integral, we treat it as a formal generator of connected Green
functions for that algebraic theory; its constructive interpretation is through
the Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction theorem applied to Euclidean
correlation functions \cite{GlimmJaffe:1987}.
\end{remark}


\begin{remark}[Real-Form Conventions]\label{rmk:RealForms}
Three distinct objects recur throughout, and we fix their names once. The real
Albert algebra $\Albert=J_3(\Oct)$ has automorphism group the compact $F_4$
and norm-preserving reduced structure group the noncompact real form
$\E_{6(-26)}$. The complexified Albert algebra $\AlbertC$ carries the minimal
$\mathbf{27}_{\mathbb C}$ of the complex group of type $\E_6$. The compact
real form, denoted $\ESix$ throughout, acts on $\AlbertC$ and on
$\mathbb P(\AlbertC)$, preserving the cubic norm $N$ and the invariant
Hermitian form; it does not preserve the real slice $\Albert$, and we never
assert that it does. All coset constructions below use the compact $\ESix$
acting on $\AlbertC$; the noncompact $\E_{6(-26)}$ plays no further role. We
also write $\SOTenXUOne$ for the maximal-rank subgroup
$\Spin(10)\cdot\UOne\subset\ESix$, globally
$(\Spin(10)\times\UOne)/\mathbb Z_4$; the spinorial $\mathbf{16}$ is a
representation of this group, not of $\SO{10}\times\UOne$ literally.
\end{remark}

No additional postulates are introduced. The compact real form \(\ESix\)
acts on \(\AlbertC\) and on its projectivization; the closed orbit of this
action is the compact Hermitian symmetric space EIII, with stabilizer
\[
  H=\SOTenXUOne,
  \qquad
  \M=\ESix/H.
\]
Inside \(\AlbertC\) this orbit is realized as the locus of rank-one elements
of fixed Hermitian norm (\autoref{thm:OrbitClass}).

UCFT is formulated as an operator-algebraic Euclidean QFT. Let
\(\mathsf{Reg}_{4}\) denote the directed set of bounded regular regions in the
reconstructed four-dimensional Riemannian spacetime. To each
\(\mathcal O\in\mathsf{Reg}_{4}\) we assign a
unital \(C^{*}\)-algebra of local observables
\[
  \mathfrak A(\mathcal O)\subset B(\mathcal H),
\]
generated by bounded functional calculi of smeared coset fields, gauge-fixed
currents, and matter fields supported in \(\mathcal O\). In the conditional
framework of this paper, the assignment obeys the standard Haag--Kastler
structural conditions
\cite{Haag1996,Araki1999,BratteliRobinson1987}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item isotony:
      \(\mathcal O_{1}\subset\mathcal O_{2}\) implies
      \(\mathfrak A(\mathcal O_{1})\subset\mathfrak A(\mathcal O_{2})\);
\item locality after Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction: observables in
      spacelike separated Lorentzian regions commute;
\item covariance under the reconstructed spacetime isometries and under the
      compact internal symmetry \(H=\SOTenXUOne\);
\item existence of a cyclic vacuum vector \(\Omega\in\mathcal H\) whose vector
      state \(\omega(A)=\langle\Omega,A\Omega\rangle\) produces the Euclidean
      Schwinger functions.
\end{enumerate}
The quasi-local observable algebra of UCFT is the inductive-limit
\(C^{*}\)-algebra
\[
  \mathfrak A
  =
  \overline{\bigcup_{\mathcal O\in\mathsf{Reg}_{4}}\mathfrak A(\mathcal O)}
  ^{\|\cdot\|}.
\]
The one-parameter unitary group in \autoref{ax:PostulateII} acts by automorphisms
\(\alpha_t(A)=U(t)AU(t)^{-1}\) of \(\mathfrak A\), and its self-adjoint
generator is the Hamiltonian of the GNS representation associated with the
vacuum state \(\omega\). Thus the functional integral used below is not an
additional postulate; it is a Euclidean device for computing the correlation
functions of this operator-algebraic theory.

For the zeta sector, the relevant object is a commutative spectral subalgebra
of the coset observable algebra. If \(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\) denotes the
positive self-adjoint coset mass operator, the algebra
\[
  C_{0}\bigl(\operatorname{Spec}(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}})\bigr)
  \subset \mathfrak A
\]
is obtained by continuous functional calculus. The Riemann-hypothesis criterion
in \autoref{sec:ZetaSector} is precisely a statement about when the arithmetic
zeta divisor is realized inside this positive spectral subalgebra.

\begin{theorem}[Jordan Structure and Dimension]\label{thm:JordanDim}
The Albert algebra
\[
  \Albert=\{X\in M_{3}(\Oct):X=X^{\dagger}\},
  \qquad
  X\circ Y=\tfrac12(XY+YX),
\]
is a $27$-dimensional real Jordan algebra; it is formally real and exceptional
\cite{Jacobson:1968,McCrimmon:2004}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Choose a basis $\{e_{0},e_{1},\dots,e_{7}\}$ of the octonions satisfying $e_{0}=1$ and $e_{i}^{2}=-1$ for $i\ge1$.
Every $X\in\Albert$ can be written uniquely as the Hermitian $3\times3$ matrix
\[
  X=\begin{pmatrix}
        \alpha_{1} & x_{3} & \bar x_{2}\\
        \bar x_{3} & \alpha_{2} & x_{1}\\
        x_{2} & \bar x_{1} & \alpha_{3}
      \end{pmatrix},
  \quad
  \alpha_{i}\in\mathbb R,
  \;
  x_{i}\in\Oct.
\]
The real-dimension count is therefore
$3\text{ scalars}+3\times8\text{ octonion components}=27$.
Jordan commutativity is obvious from the symmetric product $\circ$.
Because $\Oct$ is nonassociative, $\Albert$ is \emph{not} a subalgebra of an
associative algebra under $X\circ Y=\tfrac12(XY+YX)$, so the Jordan identity
$a\circ(b\circ a^{2})=(a\circ b)\circ a^{2}$ is not automatic. It nevertheless
holds by the Jordan--von Neumann--Wigner classification of Hermitian matrix
algebras over composition algebras: for $n\le3$ the octonionic alternative law
suffices to verify the degree-four identity on $\mathfrak h_{n}(\Oct)$, whereas
for $n\ge4$ it fails \cite{Schafer:1966,Jacobson:1968,McCrimmon:2004}. Hence
$\Albert=\mathfrak h_{3}(\Oct)$ is a $27$-dimensional formally real Jordan
algebra; being nonspecial, it is the exceptional one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Invariant Forms]\label{thm:Invs}
The real Albert algebra admits the trace form and the cubic norm
\[
  Q(X)=\Tr(X^{2}),
  \quad
  N(X)=\det_{\Oct}(X).
\]
The form $Q$ is preserved by the automorphism group $F_4$, while $N$ is
preserved by the norm-one reduced structure group, the noncompact real form
$\E_{6(-26)}$. On the complexification $\AlbertC$, the compact real form
$\ESix$ preserves the cubic norm $N$ and the Hermitian trace form
\[
  \Herm{X}{Y}=\Tr\bigl(X\circ \bar Y\bigr),
\]
where $\bar Y$ is complex conjugation with respect to the real form $\Albert$.
It preserves no complex-bilinear quadratic form, since the $\mathbf{27}$ is
not self-dual.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For $X$ as above, set
\[
  Q(X)=\Tr(X^{2}),
  \quad
  N(X)=\alpha_{1}\alpha_{2}\alpha_{3}-\sum_{i=1}^{3}\alpha_{i}\|x_{i}\|^{2}
        +2\,\Re\bigl(x_{1}x_{2}x_{3}\bigr),
\]
where juxtaposition is octonion multiplication. The form $Q$ is quadratic and
$N$ is cubic in the real coordinates. Direct calculation shows
$N(g\cdot X)=\lambda(g)N(X)$ for every $g\in\Str(\Albert)$ with multiplicative
character $\lambda(g)\in\mathbb R^{\times}$ \cite{Jacobson:1968}; the norm-one
subgroup $\{\lambda(g)=1\}$ is the noncompact real form $\E_{6(-26)}$ and
preserves $N$. The trace form $Q$ is preserved by the automorphism group
$F_4=\operatorname{Aut}(\Albert)$, which fixes the unit element and the Jordan
product; it is not preserved by the full reduced structure group, since the
$\mathbf{27}$ of $\E_6$ admits no invariant bilinear form ($\mathbf{27}$ is
not self-dual). On the complexification $\AlbertC$, the compact real form
$\ESix$ is by definition the subgroup of the complex norm-preserving group
that is unitary with respect to the Hermitian form
$\Herm{X}{Y}=\Tr(X\circ\bar Y)$; it therefore preserves $N$ and
$\Herm{\cdot}{\cdot}$ simultaneously \cite{Springer:2000}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exceptional type {\cite{Springer:2000}}]\label{thm:E6}
The reduced structure algebra of $\Albert$ has complexification of type
$\mathfrak e_{6}$. In the compact coset construction below, $\ESix$ denotes the
simply connected compact real form of this Lie type.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $\Str^{0}(\Albert)$ denote the connected component of the identity in
$\Str(\Albert)$. Jacobson's construction \cite{Jacobson:1968} realizes the
exceptional structure algebra as the sum of derivations of $\Albert$ (an
$\mathfrak f_{4}$) plus the traceless right multiplications
\[
  R_{X}: Y\mapsto X\circ Y-\frac13 Q(X,Y)\mathbf 1.
\]
After complexification this Lie algebra has type $\mathfrak e_{6}$. The main
text uses the simply connected compact real form of the same complex Lie type
for the EIII homogeneous space; this is the group denoted $\ESix$ throughout
the coset construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed EIII Orbit Criterion]\label{thm:OrbitClass}
Let $X^{\sharp}$ denote the Freudenthal adjugate on $\AlbertC$, characterized
by $X^{\sharp}=0$ exactly on the elements of Jordan rank $\le1$
\cite{Springer:2000,McCrimmon:2004}. For $v>0$ the compact $\ESix$ acts
transitively on the rank-one locus of fixed Hermitian norm,
\[
  S_{v}
  =
  \bigl\{X\in\AlbertC : X^{\sharp}=0,\ \Herm{X}{X}=v^{2}\bigr\},
\]
with vector stabilizer isomorphic to $\Spin(10)$ and line stabilizer
$H=\SOTenXUOne$. Consequently
\[
  S_{v}\cong \ESix/\Spin(10),
  \qquad
  \bigl[S_{v}\bigr]=\ESix\cdot[X_{0}]\cong\ESix/H=\M,
\]
the second orbit being the closed orbit
$\mathrm{EIII}\subset\mathbb P(\AlbertC)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Existence of an $\SOTenXUOne$ Stabilizer]
\label{cor:Isotropy}
In the projectivized complex Albert representation
\(\mathbb P(J_3(\mathbb O)_{\mathbb C})\), there exists a point
\([X_0]\) whose \(\ESix\)-stabilizer is \(\SOTenXUOne\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Let \(V=J_3(\mathbb O)_{\mathbb C}\) be the minimal \(27\)-dimensional
representation of \(\ESix\). The projectivization \(\mathbb P(V)\)
contains the closed highest-weight orbit of \(\ESix\). By the standard
description of the compact Hermitian symmetric space EIII, the stabilizer
of a highest-weight line \([X_0]\in\mathbb P(V)\) is the maximal-rank
subgroup \(\Spin(10)\cdot \UOne\), denoted in the global convention of
this paper by \(\SOTenXUOne\). Hence the orbit is
\(\ESix/(\SOTenXUOne)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of \autoref{thm:OrbitClass}]
The locus $\{X^{\sharp}=0\}\setminus\{0\}$ is the cone of rank-one elements of
$\AlbertC$, i.e.\ the affine cone over the unique closed orbit of the complex
group of type $\E_6$ in $\mathbb P(\AlbertC)$ (the complexified Cayley plane)
\cite{Springer:2000}. For a linear action of a compact group whose
complexification acts on $\mathbb P(V)$, the closed complex orbit is a single
orbit of the compact real form: it is the orbit of the highest-weight line
\cite{Helgason:1978}. Hence $\ESix$ acts transitively on rank-one lines, and
by \autoref{cor:Isotropy} the stabilizer of a line is the maximal-rank
subgroup $H=\SOTenXUOne$; the orbit of lines is
$\mathrm{EIII}=\ESix/H$.

Under $H$ the $\mathbf{27}$ branches as
$\mathbf{16}_{+1}\oplus\mathbf{10}_{-2}\oplus\mathbf{1}_{+4}$, and the
highest-weight line is the $\Spin(10)$-singlet direction $\mathbf{1}_{+4}$,
which carries nonzero $\UOne_\psi$ charge. Therefore $\Spin(10)$ fixes a
highest-weight vector $X_{0}$ pointwise, while the $\UOne_\psi$ factor acts on
the line $\mathbb C X_{0}$ through a nontrivial character and hence acts
transitively on the circle of vectors of fixed Hermitian norm in that line.
Combining the two transitivities, $\ESix$ acts transitively on $S_{v}$, with
vector stabilizer isomorphic to $\Spin(10)$; thus
$S_{v}\cong\ESix/\Spin(10)$, of dimension $78-45=33$, a circle bundle over
$\mathrm{EIII}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:OrbitInv}
If $g\in \ESix$ and $X\in\AlbertC$ then
$\Herm{gX}{gX}=\Herm{X}{X}$ and $\Herm{(gX)^{\sharp}}{(gX)^{\sharp}}
=\Herm{X^{\sharp}}{X^{\sharp}}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Invariance of the Hermitian form is \autoref{thm:Invs}. The adjugate satisfies
the structure-group equivariance $(gX)^{\sharp}=g^{\sharp}(X^{\sharp})$, where
$g\mapsto g^{\sharp}$ is the dual (contragredient) representation on
$\overline{\mathbf{27}}$ \cite{Springer:2000,McCrimmon:2004}; the compact form
acts unitarily on the dual as well, so the Hermitian norm of $X^{\sharp}$ is
invariant.
\end{proof}

Fix $v>0$, set $c_{2}=v^{2}$, and choose a rank-one element
$X_{0}\in S_{v}$ (\autoref{thm:OrbitClass}). Define, for $X\in\AlbertC$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Pdef}
\begin{aligned}
  P(X)
  &=\Herm{X^{\sharp}}{X^{\sharp}}
  +\bigl[\Herm{X}{X}-c_{2}\bigr]^{2},\\[2pt]
  V(X)
  &=P(X)
  +\alpha\,\bigl[\Herm{X}{X}-c_{2}\bigr]^{k},
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
with $\alpha>0$ and even $k\ge2$. Both summands are $\ESix$-invariant: the
Hermitian form is invariant by \autoref{thm:Invs}, and the adjugate
intertwines the $\mathbf{27}$ with its dual $\overline{\mathbf{27}}$
equivariantly, on both of which the compact form acts unitarily
\cite{Springer:2000}, so $\Herm{X^{\sharp}}{X^{\sharp}}$ is invariant
(\autoref{lem:OrbitInv}).

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:Zeros}
$P(X)=0$ if and only if $X\in S_{v}=\ESix\cdot X_{0}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$P(X)=0$ forces $X^{\sharp}=0$ and $\Herm{X}{X}=c_{2}$, i.e.\
$X\in S_{v}$; transitivity on $S_{v}$ is \autoref{thm:OrbitClass}.
Conversely, $P$ vanishes on $S_{v}$ by $\ESix$-invariance of both summands.
\end{proof}
\begin{theorem}[Rigidity of the Symmetry-Breaking Orbit]
\label{thm:Breaking}
The polynomial $V(X)$ in \eqref{eq:Pdef} is $\ESix$-invariant, coercive, and
attains its absolute minimum precisely on the rank-one orbit
\[
  S_{v}=\ESix\cdot X_{0}
  \cong
  \ESix/\Spin(10),
\]
a $\UOne$ fibration over the coset $\M=\MCosetInline$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Invariance was established above. Since $\Herm{X}{X}=\|X\|^{2}$ is positive
definite on $\AlbertC$, the even-degree term
$\alpha[\Herm{X}{X}-c_{2}]^{k}$ dominates as $\|X\|\to\infty$, so $V$ is
coercive. By \autoref{lem:Zeros}, $P\ge0$ with $P=0$ exactly on
$S_{v}$; the additional term is nonnegative and vanishes on the same
level set. Hence $V=0$ precisely on the orbit and is positive elsewhere.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dimension Count]\label{thm:DimCount}
\[
  \dim \ESix=78,
  \quad
  \dim (\SOTenXUOne)=46,
  \quad
  \dim \left(\MCoset\right)=32.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The dimension of $\ESix$ is tabulated in \cite{Slansky:1981}.  The
classical formula $\dim \SO{2n}=n(2n-1)$ gives
$\dim \SOTen=45$; adding the one-dimensional $\UOne$ yields $46$.  The
difference is $32$. Correspondingly $\dim S_{v}=78-45=33$: the rank-one orbit
of \autoref{thm:Breaking} is a circle bundle over $\M$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Vacuum Manifold]\label{cor:VacuumManifold}
On the minimizing orbit $S_{v}$ the breaking pattern of the global symmetry is
$\ESix\to\Spin(10)$, with $33$ Goldstone directions. The fiber direction is
the phase of the line $\mathbb C\,X_{0}$, on which the $\UOne_\psi\subset H$
acts through a nontrivial character; in the representation used from
\autoref{sec:sigma} onward, $H$ (and in particular $\UOne_\psi$) is gauged,
and this single mode is absorbed by the $\UOne_\psi$ gauge field through the
St\"uckelberg mechanism (cf.\ \autoref{sec:Discussion}). The physical vacuum
manifold is the orbit of complex rays,
\[
  \mathcal M
  =\ESix\cdot[X_{0}]
  =\MCoset,
\]
carrying the remaining $32$ Goldstone bosons.
\end{corollary}

Throughout this paper, we denote by $\M$ the coset manifold $\ESix/(\SOTenXUOne)$.
\end{remark}

\section{Emergent Spacetime and Field Content}
\label{sec:EmergentFields}
%======================================================================%
The vacuum manifold $\M$ is 32-dimensional, but a spacetime-filling field
configuration immerses a four-dimensional world manifold into it. In this
section, we show how a frame and a nondegenerate Riemannian metric arise as the
first fundamental form of that immersion, how physical Lorentzian spacetime is
recovered by continuation, and how the transverse directions combine with
$\SOTenXUOne$ gauge connections and fermionic coherent states to furnish the
matter content.

Choose a local coordinate patch
$$
  \{\phi^{\alpha} (x)\}_{\alpha=0}^{31},\quad
  \phi^{\alpha}(x):\mathbb R^{4}\to\mathcal M,
$$
where $\{x^{\mu}\}_{\mu=0}^3$ are smooth parameters on the world
manifold.  Write $\theta=g^{-1}\mathrm d g$ for the left-invariant
Maurer-Cartan one-form on $\ESix$ and let $\theta^{ a}_{\alpha}$ denote its components along the broken generators $\mathfrak e_{6}\setminus(\mathfrak{so}(10)\oplus\mathfrak u(1))$.

\begin{remark}
Throughout this section, the symbols $x^{\mu}$ serve solely as four real parameters that label the field configuration $\phi^{\alpha}(x)$. Their physical interpretation as spacetime coordinates is justified only after \autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}, where the pull-back metric is shown to be non-degenerate, together with the continuation to Lorentzian signature discussed there.
\end{remark}

Let $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}=f^{2}(-K_{\alpha\beta})$ denote the unique
positive-definite $\ESix$-invariant metric on the broken subspace $\mathfrak m$
(\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}), where $K_{\alpha\beta}$ is the restriction of the
Killing form; on the compact form $-K|_{\mathfrak m}$ is positive definite.

\begin{lemma}[Rank-Four Embedding]\label{lem:Rank4}
Let the pull-back
$g_{\mu\nu}=\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}\,\partial_\mu\phi^{\alpha}\,\partial_\nu\phi^{\beta}$
be non-degenerate at $x$. Then the differential
$\mathrm d\phi_{x}:T_{x}\mathbb R^{4}\to\mathfrak m$ has rank four, and its
image $\mathfrak p_{0}(x)=\Im\,\mathrm d\phi_{x}$ is a four-dimensional
subspace of $\mathfrak m$; the transverse $28$ Goldstone directions span its
$\mathfrak g$-orthogonal complement $\mathfrak p_{m}(x)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since $\dim T_{x}\mathbb R^{4}=4$, one has
$\operatorname{rank}\mathrm d\phi_{x}\le4$, and because $\mathfrak g$ is
positive definite $\operatorname{rank} g_{\mu\nu}=\operatorname{rank}\mathrm
d\phi_{x}$. Non-degeneracy of $g_{\mu\nu}$ therefore forces
$\operatorname{rank}\mathrm d\phi_{x}=4$, i.e.\ $\mathrm d\phi_{x}$ is injective.
\end{proof}

Choosing a $\mathfrak g$-orthonormal basis of $\mathfrak p_{0}(x)$ and pulling
back the Maurer-Cartan form gives the tetrad
\begin{equation}\label{eq:eDef}
  e_\mu^{ a}(x)=
  \theta^{ a}_{\alpha}\bigl(\phi(x)\bigr)\,
  \partial_\mu\phi^{ \alpha}(x),
  \qquad a=0,\dots,3,
\end{equation}
which by \autoref{lem:Rank4} is invertible for a non-degenerate configuration.
The induced (first fundamental) metric is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:InducedMetric}
  g_{\mu\nu}(x)
  =\delta_{ab}\,e_\mu^{ a}(x)\,e_\nu^{ b}(x),
\end{equation}
where $\delta_{ab}$ is the restriction of the positive-definite $\mathfrak g$ to
the orthonormal frame $\mathfrak p_{0}(x)$.

\begin{theorem}[Emergent Riemannian Geometry]\label{thm:EmergentRiemannian}
For an open dense set of spacetime-filling configurations
$\phi:\mathbb R^{4}\to\M$, the pull-back \eqref{eq:InducedMetric} is a
smooth, non-degenerate, positive-definite (Riemannian) metric on
$\mathbb R^{4}$. It is the first fundamental form of the immersion
$\phi$ into the Hermitian symmetric space $\M$, and no metric is postulated on
$\mathbb R^{4}$ independently of $\phi$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Non-degeneracy and rank four are
\autoref{lem:Rank4}; positive-definiteness is inherited from that of
$\mathfrak g$ (\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}), since
$g_{\mu\nu}v^{\mu}v^{\nu}=\mathfrak g\bigl(\mathrm d\phi_x v,\mathrm
d\phi_x v\bigr)\ge0$ with equality iff $v=0$.
\end{proof}

The tetrad $e_\mu^{ a}$ is thereby the fundamental gravitational field, and
its dynamics follow from the $\sigma$-model action of \autoref{sec:sigma}.

\begin{remark}[Recovery of Lorentzian Spacetime]\label{rmk:Continuation}
The coset metric $\mathfrak g$ is positive definite, so the induced geometry of
\autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian} is necessarily Riemannian: a pull-back of a
definite form cannot have indefinite signature. This is the appropriate object,
because the Schwinger functions of \autoref{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB} are
Euclidean. Physical Lorentzian spacetime is recovered from those Euclidean
correlation functions by Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction
\cite{GlimmJaffe:1987}, equivalently by Wick rotation of the reconstructed
frame, exactly as in Euclidean quantum gravity.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Emergent Spacetime and Rigidity]\label{rmk:Emergent}
The image of $\mathbb R^{4}$ inside $\M$ is the physical spacetime
manifold, and its metric \eqref{eq:InducedMetric} emerges from the field
configuration rather than being put in by hand. The four frame directions
$\mathfrak p_{0}(x)$ are not an $H$-invariant subspace: by
\autoref{lem:Irrep} the isotropy representation on $\mathfrak m$ is irreducible,
so $\mathfrak m$ admits no $H$-invariant four-plane. The splitting
$\mathfrak m=\mathfrak p_{0}\oplus\mathfrak p_{m}$ is therefore selected
spontaneously by the configuration $\phi(x)$, in the same sense that a
vacuum expectation value selects a direction; there is no contradiction between
the emergence of a preferred frame and the $H$-irreducibility of the coset
scalars.
\end{remark}

With the vierbein $e_{\mu}^{a}(x)$ invertible and
\autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian} establishing a non-degenerate induced metric
(Lorentzian after continuation, \autoref{rmk:Continuation}),
$\mathbb R^{4}$ acquires the interpretation of physical spacetime.  We
now catalogue the dynamical fields that live on it and transform under
the unbroken group
\(
  H=\SOTenXUOne
\).

Introduce the connection one-form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:GaugeConnection}
  A_{\mu}(x)=A_{\mu}^{A}(x)\,T_{A}\in\mathfrak h,
\end{equation}
which transforms by the affine shift
\(
  A_{\mu}\mapsto h\,A_{\mu}\,h^{-1}+h\,\partial_{\mu}h^{-1}
\)
for $h(x)\in H$.  The adjoint of $\mathfrak h$ contains
$45$ generators of $\mathfrak{so}(10)$ and one of $\mathfrak u(1)$,
hence
\(
  N_{\mathrm{gauge}}=46
\)
gauge bosons. The $45$ $\Spin(10)$ bosons are massless, each with two physical
polarizations; the $\UOne_\psi$ boson acquires a St\"uckelberg mass once the

\autoref{sec:Discussion}).

The truncation contains a single left-handed Weyl multiplet in the chiral

\(
  \mathbf{16}_{+1}
\)
of $\Spin(10)$; all fermions are sections of the bundle
\(
  S\otimes\mathcal V_{\mathbf{16}}
\),
where $S$ is the Weyl spin bundle determined by the emergent vierbein
\cite{Baez:2010}. Thus $n_{F}=16$ denotes the $\Spin(10)$ multiplicity,
corresponding to $32$ real fermionic components, in the beta-function

The $32$ coordinates of $\M$ form a single real-irreducible multiplet of

\(
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}.
\)
There is thus no $H$-invariant $4+28$ decomposition; the four frame directions

(\autoref{rmk:Emergent}). Relative to a given background, the four longitudinal

$\mathfrak p_{m}$ acquire a mass of order $f\mu$

\(

real scalars enter the functional renormalization-group flow of

\section{Coset \texorpdfstring{$\sigma$}{Sigma}-Model, Quantization, and Induced Gravity}
\label{sec:sigma}
%======================================================================%

This section works in the coset representation of the observable net introduced
in \autoref{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB}. The $\sigma$-model with target \(\M\)
specifies the local fields whose bounded functional calculi generate the coset,
gauge, and matter subalgebras. Its Euclidean path integral is used as a
computational representation of the corresponding Schwinger functions. Within
this representation we derive the one-loop Einstein--Hilbert term induced by
matter fluctuations and identify the coset mass spectrum. The Planck scale is
then set by the $\sigma$-model decay constant and the ultraviolet
regularization.

Let $g(x)\in \ESix$ be a local coset representative.  
The left-invariant Maurer-Cartan form is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:MC}
  \theta_\mu(x) = g^{-1}(x)\,\partial_\mu g(x) \in \mathfrak e_{6}.
\end{equation}
Decomposing $\theta_\mu$ into unbroken $\mathfrak h = \mathfrak{so}(10) \oplus \mathfrak u(1)$ and broken $\mathfrak m$ components gives
\[
  \theta_\mu = A_\mu^{ i} H_i + e_\mu^{ \alpha} X_\alpha,
\]
with $H_i \in \mathfrak h$, $X_\alpha \in \mathfrak m$, and coefficient functions $A_\mu^{ i}(x)$ and $e_\mu^{ \alpha}(x)$. $\alpha,\beta$ run over the $32$ broken generators while $i,j$ label the $45$ generators of $\mathfrak h$.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:Irrep}
The isotropy representation of
$H = \SOTenXUOne$ on the broken subspace
$\mathfrak m$ is irreducible.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
As an $H$-module, $\mathfrak m_{\mathbb C}\cong
\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$ \cite{Helgason:1978,Slansky:1981}.
Each summand is irreducible, and the two are exchanged by complex conjugation.
A proper nonzero $H$-invariant real subspace of $\mathfrak m$ would complexify
to a conjugation-stable invariant subspace of
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$; the only invariant
subspaces are $0$, the two summands, and the whole space, and the summands are
not conjugation-stable. Hence $\mathfrak m$ is irreducible as a real
$H$-module. It is of complex type: its real commutant is
$\mathbb C=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf 1,J\}$, where $J$ is the invariant
complex structure of the Hermitian symmetric space EIII.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:uniqueMetric}
Up to an overall positive constant $f^{2}$, there exists a unique
$\ESix$-invariant symmetric bilinear form $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}$ on
$\mathfrak m$, namely
$\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta} = f^{2}\,(-K_{\alpha\beta})$, where
$K_{\alpha\beta}$ is the restriction to $\mathfrak m$ of the Killing form on
$\mathfrak e_{6}$. On the compact real form of $\ESix$ the Killing form is
negative definite, so $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}$ is positive definite.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See appendix for a short Schur-lemma argument.
\end{proof}

By the coset construction of nonlinear realizations \cite{CWZ69}, the
broken generators \(X_{\alpha}\) furnish local coordinates
\(\phi^{\alpha}(x)\) on the vacuum manifold
\(\M\).  The target-space metric
\(\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}\) is the unique \(\ESix\)-invariant metric
(\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}), and the spacetime metric \(g_{\mu\nu}\)
emerges from the vierbein pull-back construction of spacetime.
We introduce the \(\mathfrak h\)-valued gauge connection
\(A_{\mu}=A_{\mu}^{i}\,T_{i}\), and define
\[
  D_{\mu}\phi^{\alpha}
    = \partial_{\mu}\phi^{\alpha}
      + A_{\mu}^{i}(t_{i})^{\alpha}{}_{\beta}\,\phi^{\beta},
  \qquad
  F_{\mu\nu}
    = \partial_{\mu}A_{\nu}
      - \partial_{\nu}A_{\mu}
      + [A_{\mu},A_{\nu}],
\]
where \((t_{i})^{\alpha}{}_{\beta}\) are the representation
matrices of \(\mathfrak h\) on the coset coordinates.

By standard effective-field-theory arguments \cite{CWZ69,Weinberg:1996kr}, the
low-energy action is organized as a derivative expansion constrained by the
symmetries. In the minimal two-derivative truncation, with local \(H\) and
Lorentz invariance imposed and with a single left-handed Weyl fermion
\(\psi(x)\) in the \(\mathbf{16}_{+1}\) of \(\Spin(10)\), we use
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigmaAction}
\begin{aligned}
  S[\phi,\,A,\,\psi]
  = \int d^{4}x\,\sqrt{-g}\,\biggl[
    & \frac12\,g^{\mu\nu}\,\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}\,
      D_{\mu}\phi^{\alpha}\,D_{\nu}\phi^{\beta}
    -\frac14\,g^{-2}\,\Tr\bigl(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}\bigr)\\
    & + \bar\psi\,\mathrm i\,\gamma^{\mu}D_{\mu}\psi
    -y\,\bar\psi\,\phi^{\alpha}T_{\alpha}\psi
  \biggr].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The dimensionless couplings \(g\) and \(y\), together with the decay
constant \(f\), depend on the renormalization scale; their flow is
studied in \autoref{sec:FRG}. Equation \eqref{eq:sigmaAction} is displayed in
Lorentzian signature; the Euclidean functional integral \eqref{eq:pathIntegral}
employs its Wick-rotated form \(S_{\mathrm E}\), obtained by continuation onto
the Riemannian induced background of \autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}
(\(\sqrt{-g}\to\sqrt{g}\),
\(\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}S}\to\mathrm{e}^{-S_{\mathrm E}}\)), in accordance with
\autoref{rmk:Continuation}. The Yukawa term is schematic: for a single
\(\mathbf{16}_{+1}\) there is no \(\Spin(10)\)-invariant cubic
\(\bar\psi\phi\psi\), since neither \(\mathbf{16}\) nor \(\overline{\mathbf{16}}\)
occurs in \(\mathbf{16}\otimes\mathbf{16}
=\mathbf{10}\oplus\mathbf{120}\oplus\mathbf{126}\); it is defined only on
completion of the matter to a full \(\mathbf{27}\), or replaced by the standard
derivative coupling of matter to the Goldstone current, and plays no role in
the coset kinematics or the induced-gravity result below.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:positivity}
For $f^{2}>0$, the Euclidean continuation of the scalar kinetic term in
\eqref{eq:sigmaAction} is positive definite.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Since $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}$ is positive definite on the broken subspace, 
\(
  D_\mu\phi^{\alpha}\,D^\mu\phi^{\beta}\,
  \mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}\ge 0,
\)
with equality only for vanishing covariant derivative.
\end{proof}

The nonlinear sigma model action \eqref{eq:sigmaAction}, continued to Euclidean
signature, determines the Euclidean Schwinger functions of the coset
representation. Gauge redundancy under local \(\SOTenXUOne\) is fixed by the
Faddeev-Popov procedure \cite{FP67}, and the formal generating functional is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pathIntegral}
  Z =
  \int \mathcal D\phi\,\mathcal D A\,\mathcal D\psi\;
    \exp\bigl[-S_{\mathrm E}[\phi,\,A,\,\psi]\bigr].
\end{equation}
When these Schwinger functions satisfy reflection positivity, Euclidean
invariance, symmetry, and clustering, Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction
recovers the Hilbert-space representation and local observable net described
in \autoref{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB}
\cite{GlimmJaffe:1987,Haag1996}.

Expanding around a slowly varying background \(X_{0}\in\mathcal M\) and
integrating out the quadratic fluctuations by Gaussian functional
integration yields the one-loop effective action \cite{Peskin:1995ev}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:effAction}
  \Gamma^{(1)}
  = \frac12\,\log\det\bigl(-\Delta_{\phi}\bigr)
    - \log\det\bigl(-\Delta_{\mathrm{ghost}}\bigr)
    + \cdots,
\end{equation}
where \(-\Delta_{\phi}\) and \(-\Delta_{\mathrm{ghost}}\) are the
second-variation operators for the physical and ghost fields,
respectively. Its quadratic expansion in the fluctuation \(\varphi=X-X_{0}\in T_{X_{0}}\,\mathcal M\) reads
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:OneLoopQuadratic}
  \Gamma^{(1)}[X_{0}+\varphi]
  = \Gamma^{(1)}[X_{0}]
    + \frac12\int d^{4}x\;\varphi^{I}\,
      \bigl[\Hess V(X_{0})\bigr]_{IJ}\,
      \varphi^{J}
    + \cdots,
\end{equation}
so that the eigenvalues of the Hessian of the effective potential
govern the one-loop mass spectrum (\autoref{thm:Mass28}). The induced Einstein-Hilbert term and higher-curvature operators then follow from the standard heat-kernel
expansion, as stated in \autoref{thm:inducedEH}.


\begin{remark}
We evaluate all functional determinants in Euclidean signature on a
four-torus $\mathbb T^{4}$ of finite volume $V$ and later take the
thermodynamic limit $V\to\infty$. The existence and local form of the induced
Einstein--Hilbert term follow from the heat-kernel expansion; its renormalized
coefficient is regulator-, truncation-, and spectrum-dependent.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Induced Gravity]\label{thm:inducedEH}
One-loop quantum fluctuations induce an Einstein-Hilbert term
\begin{equation}\label{eq:EH}
  \Gamma^{ (1)} \supset \frac12 M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{ 2} \int d^{4}x\, \sqrt{-g}\, R,
\end{equation}
generated radiatively rather than postulated. The contribution of the
$N_{s}=32$ Goldstone scalars alone is
\[
  M_{\mathrm{Pl},\,\mathrm{scalar}}^{2}
  = \frac{N_{s} f^{2}}{(4\pi)^{2}} \log\frac{\Lambda^{2}}{\mu^{2}}>0,
\]
with $\Lambda$ the cutoff and $\mu$ the renormalization scale. The full
coefficient receives additional contributions from the Weyl and gauge sectors
(\autoref{tab:SDWcoeffs}), whose relative sign is scheme dependent; the
invariant content of the theorem is the one-loop origin of the term, not the
numerical value of $M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See \autoref{app:Gravity}, which follows Sakharov's induced-gravity
argument and employs the heat-kernel coefficients listed in
\autoref{tab:SDWcoeffs}. The numerical coefficient in \eqref{eq:EH} is
truncation- and scheme-dependent; the invariant content of the theorem is the
one-loop origin of the term. The scalar-sector contribution is positive, while
the sign of the full coefficient depends on the Weyl and gauge sectors and is
scheme-dependent.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Mass Spectrum]
\label{thm:Mass28}
The one-loop effective potential is $H$-invariant, and $\mathfrak m$ is
$H$-irreducible (\autoref{lem:Irrep}); hence, by Schur's lemma, its Hessian at
the vacuum is a multiple of the identity,
\[
  \Hess V(X_{0})=m^{2}\,\mathbf 1_{32}.
\]
If the one-loop correction is stabilizing, so that $m^{2}>0$, the common
(pseudo-Goldstone) mass is $m^{2}=f^{2}\mu^{2}$ in the normalization of
\eqref{eq:sigmaAction}.
On a spacetime-filling background the four longitudinal directions
$\mathfrak p_{0}$ of \autoref{lem:Rank4} are the tetrad (gravitational) degrees
of freedom; the remaining $28$ transverse fluctuations $\mathfrak p_{m}$ are
physical scalars of common mass $m^{2}=f^{2}\mu^{2}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
See appendix. The tree potential of \autoref{thm:Breaking}
is $\ESix$-invariant and vanishes on the entire orbit $\M$, so all $32$ coset
scalars are exact Goldstone bosons at tree level. The gauge and Yukawa
couplings of \eqref{eq:sigmaAction} respect only $H$, so the one-loop effective
potential is $H$-invariant but not $\ESix$-invariant and lifts these
pseudo-Goldstones. Its Hessian commutes with $H$; since $\mathfrak m$ is
$H$-irreducible (\autoref{lem:Irrep}), Schur's lemma forces
$\Hess V(X_{0})=m^{2}\mathbf 1_{32}$ with a single
$m^{2}=f^{2}\mu^{2}>0$ fixed by the flow. On a non-degenerate background the frame
condensate $\langle\partial_\mu\phi^{a}\rangle=e_\mu^{a}$ reinterprets
the four directions $\mathfrak p_{0}$ as the tetrad, promoted to the
gravitational field by the induced Einstein-Hilbert term of
\autoref{thm:inducedEH}; the $28$ transverse fluctuations remain as physical
scalars, each of mass $m^{2}=f^{2}\mu^{2}$.
\end{proof}

The coefficient $a_{4}$ in the heat-kernel expansion generates
\[
  \alpha R^{2} + \beta R_{\mu\nu} R^{\mu\nu}
  + \gamma R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}.
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{prop:irrelevant}
In the truncation of \autoref{sec:FRG}, the dimensionless couplings
$\alpha,\beta,\gamma$ scale as $k^{-2}$ at large momentum scale $k$; the
corresponding operators are irrelevant within the effective coset flow below
the cutoff \(\epsilon<1/6\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $a_{4} \propto f^{0}$ while $M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2} \propto f^{2}$, one has $\alpha \sim a_{4} / M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2} \propto k^{-2}$; the same reasoning applies to $\beta$ and $\gamma$.
\end{proof}

The construction contains a single dimensionful input: the Goldstone decay
constant $f$ that normalizes the invariant metric of
\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}. Every other scale is derived. The Planck mass is
not postulated but generated radiatively by \autoref{thm:inducedEH}, with the
one-loop enhancement factor $N_{s}\log(\Lambda^{2}/\mu^{2})/(4\pi)^{2}$
placing $f$ within roughly an order of magnitude of $M_{\mathrm{Pl}}$ itself.
The grand-unified scale is the heterotic matching scale of
\autoref{sec:FRG}, fixed by the crossing of the running gauge
couplings rather than by any parameter of the coset action. The electroweak
scale enters only through the subsequent
$\SOTen\to\SU{5}\to\SMSubgroup$ descent
(\autoref{cor:BreakingChain}) and is not determined by the truncation studied
here. The resulting hierarchy is:

\[
\begin{array}{lcc}
\hline
\text{Scale} & \text{Definition} & \hspace{10pt}\text{Order of Magnitude} \\
\hline
\text{Goldstone decay constant} & f & \text{input} \\
\text{Planck mass} & M_{\mathrm{Pl}} & \gtrsim 10^{18}\,\text{GeV} \\
\text{Heterotic GUT matching scale} & M_{\mathrm{GUT}} & \simeq 2\times10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}\ \\
\text{Electroweak VEV} & v & 246\,\text{GeV} \\
\hline
\end{array}
\]

The $28$ transverse coset scalars of \autoref{thm:Mass28}, with common mass of
order $f\mu$, sit at the ultraviolet end of this hierarchy and communicate
with the lower scales only through Planck-suppressed operators; their role in
what follows is structural, as the source of the induced gravitational
dynamics, rather than phenomenological.

\section{Functional Renormalization-Group Flow}
\label{sec:FRG}
%======================================================================%

We now study the coset-field theory under continuous coarse-graining. After
introducing the functional renormalization-group (RG) machinery and the
dimensionless couplings, we compute the one- and two-loop $\beta$-functions in
a finite truncation, identify the domain in which that truncation is valid, and
determine the grand-unified matching scale.

We work with the Wetterich equation
\[
  \partial_{k}\Gamma_{k}
  =\frac12\Tr\bigl[
    \bigl(\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}+R_{k}\bigr)^{-1}
    \partial_{k}R_{k}\bigr],
\]
choosing Litim's regulator
\(
  R_{k}(p^{2})=Z_{k}(k^{2}-p^{2})
  \theta(k^{2}-p^{2})
\),
which optimizes threshold integrals while preserving locality. The natural
dimensionless
couplings are
\[
  \xi=\frac{k^{2}}{f^{2}},\quad
  \hat g^{2}=\frac{k^{2}g^{2}}{f^{2}},\quad
  \hat y^{2}=\frac{k^{2}y^{2}}{f^{2}},\quad
  \kappa=\frac{k^{2}}{M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}}.

We also introduce the coset loop-expansion parameter

which controls the derivative expansion of the nonlinear $\sigma$-model; the

\begin{remark}[Validity Boundary]\label{rmk:EpsilonCutoff}
The coset RG equations in this section are used only in the effective regime

  \epsilon<\frac16 .
\]
At \(\epsilon=1/6\), the local coset description reaches the matching boundary

is interpreted as a signal that the effective coset variables are no longer the
correct coordinates beyond the cutoff, provided the heterotic matching

\end{remark}

At one loop, the running of the gauge, scalar, and gravitational
couplings is determined by the field content listed in
\autoref{tab:FieldContent}.  Working in the minimal-subtraction scheme
(or, equivalently, in the functional RG, with a Litim regulator), the
standard group-theoretic result for a gauge algebra $\mathfrak g$ with
$n_{F}$ Weyl fermions and $n_{S}$ real scalars in the fundamental
representation reads \cite{MachacekVaughn:1984}  
\[
  \beta_{g^{-2}}
  =
  \frac{b_0}{48\pi^2},\qquad b_0 = \frac{11}{3}\,C_{A}
  -\frac{4}{3}\,T_{R}n_{F}
  -\frac16\,T_{R}n_{S}.
\]
Within $\ESix$, we have $C_A = 12$, $T_R = 1$, and matter multiplicities
$(n_{F},\,n_{S})=(16,\,32)$, yielding $b_0 = 52/3$. These coefficients are
convention- and scheme-dependent bookkeeping for the printed reduced
$\beta$-system: the value mixes the $\ESix$ normalization $C_A=12$ with a unit
matter index $T_R=1$, and the coupling that runs below the coset scale is that
of the unbroken $H=\SOTenXUOne$ rather than $\ESix$. Only the qualitative
structure of the flow---not the numerical value or a convention-independent
sign of $b_0$---enters what follows.

Throughout the paper, we trade the gauge coupling
$g^{2}(k)$ for the canonically rescaled quantity
\[
  \hat g^{2}(k)=\frac{k^{2}}{f^{2}}\,g^{2}(k).
\]
Its full RG flow separates into canonical scaling and loop-induced running,
\[
  \partial_t\hat g^{2}
  =
  2\hat g^{2}
  +\beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}},
  \qquad
  \beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}}
  =
  -\frac{b_0}{24\pi^{2}}\,\hat g^{4}.
\]
The fixed-point audit below concerns this reduced loop system rather than the
canonical scaling term itself.

The coset-field wave-function $f^{2}$ is protected by the
$\ESix$ symmetry at this order, hence $\beta_{f^{2}}=0$.
The dimensionful ratio
\(
  \xi(k)=k^{2}/f^{2}
\)
therefore runs only through its canonical dimension:
\(
  \beta_{\xi}=2\xi.
\)

Finally, integrating out the $28$ massive coset fields generates the
one-loop Sakharov flow of the induced Planck mass
\cite{SakharovInducedGravity,AdlerInducedGravity}:\footnote{%
  The minus sign follows from
  $\mu\,\partial_{\mu}\log(\Lambda^{2}/\mu^{2})=-2$.}
\[
  \beta_{\kappa}
  = 2\kappa
    +\frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\kappa^{2},
  \qquad
  \kappa=\frac{k^{2}}{M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}(k)}.
\]
Collecting the three directions, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:OneLoop}
    \beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}}
      =-\frac{b_{0}}{24\pi^{2}}\,\hat g^{4},\qquad
    \beta_{\xi}=2\xi,\qquad
    \beta_{\kappa}=2\kappa+\frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\kappa^{2}.
\end{equation}

Coset-scalar self-interactions and Yukawa diagrams enter at two loops. Keeping
the leading structures in the truncation gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:TwoLoop}
\begin{aligned}
\beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}}
 &= -\frac{b_{0}}{24\pi^{2}}\hat g^{4}
   -\frac{b_{1}}{(16\pi^{2})^{2}}\hat g^{6}
   +\frac{c_{1}}{16\pi^{2}}\hat g^{4}\hat y^{2},
\\[6pt]
\beta_{\hat y^{2}}
 &= b_{y} \hat y^{4}
    + c_{2}\hat g^{2}\hat y^{2},\\[6pt]
\beta_{\xi}
 &= 2\xi + A\,\hat y^{2}\,\xi + b_{0}\,\hat g^{2}\,\xi.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The coefficients $b_{1}$, $c_{1}$, $c_{2}$, $b_{y}$, and $A$ are fixed by the
field content of \autoref{tab:FieldContent}; their values, together with the
projection rules used to extract them from the Wetterich equation, are derived
in \autoref{app:FRG} and collected in \autoref{tab:TwoLoopCoeffs}. Two sign
facts control everything that follows: $b_{0},b_{1}>0$, so both pure-gauge
terms drive $\hat g^{2}$ toward asymptotic freedom, while $c_{1}<0$, so the
Yukawa correction cannot balance them at positive coupling. These signs are
the entire input to the fixed-point analysis of the next subsection.

\begin{proposition}[No Positive-Coupling Fixed Point of the Reduced Loop System]
\label{thm:FixedPoint}
For the reduced loop $\beta$-system \eqref{eq:TwoLoop} with the coefficients of
\autoref{tab:TwoLoopCoeffs}, the only nontrivial real gauge--Yukawa branch has
\(
  \hat g^{2}_{\ast}<0
\)
and is therefore outside the physical positive-coupling domain. The physical
coset flow below the cutoff \(\epsilon<1/6\) is consequently an effective
flow, not an interacting asymptotic-safety completion. At the cutoff boundary
the ultraviolet description is supplied by the
\(\E_{8}\times E_{8}\) heterotic theory.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Insert the coefficients of \autoref{tab:TwoLoopCoeffs} into
\eqref{eq:TwoLoop}. For \(\hat g^{2}>0\) and \(\hat y^{2}>0\), every term in
\[
\beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}}
 = -\frac{b_{0}}{24\pi^{2}}\hat g^{4}
   -\frac{b_{1}}{(16\pi^{2})^{2}}\hat g^{6}
   +\frac{c_{1}}{16\pi^{2}}\hat g^{4}\hat y^{2}
\]
is strictly negative, since \(b_{0},b_{1}>0\) and \(c_{1}<0\). Hence
\(\beta_{\hat g^{2}}^{\mathrm{loop}}\) cannot vanish in the positive-coupling interior.
Solving the algebraic system gives the nontrivial real branch
\[
  \hat g^{2}_{\ast}\simeq -0.567,
  \qquad
  \hat y^{2}_{\ast}\simeq -1.70,
\]
which is not a physical coupling branch. The \(\kappa\)-flow
\[
  \beta_{\kappa}=2\kappa+\frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\kappa^{2}
\]
has its nontrivial zero at negative \(\kappa\), while the positive-\(\kappa\)
flow is repulsive toward the heterotic matching boundary. This is consistent
with \autoref{rmk:EpsilonCutoff}: the coset truncation is not required to
provide its own interacting ultraviolet fixed point beyond \(\epsilon=1/6\).
\end{proof}

Running the gauge couplings downward,
\(M_{\mathrm{GUT}}\) is defined by the crossing
\(
  \alpha_{2}^{-1}(k)=\alpha_{1}^{-1}(k)
\).
Using supersymmetric/heterotic-threshold one-loop slopes and standard
electroweak inputs at $M_{Z}$,
\(
  M_{\mathrm{GUT}}\simeq2\times10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}
\),
with a $5\%$ uncertainty from two-loop and threshold effects.
With the plain non-supersymmetric Standard-Model one-loop coefficients
\(b_{1}=41/10\) and \(b_{2}=-19/6\), the same crossing occurs near
\(10^{13}\,\mathrm{GeV}\). Thus the scale quoted here is a heterotic matching
scale, not a prediction of the nonsupersymmetric coset truncation alone.

\section{The Coset Spectral Zeta Sector}
\label{sec:ZetaSector}
%======================================================================%

The zeta function enters UCFT through a commutative spectral subalgebra of the
coset observable algebra. The object on the UCFT side is the nonnegative
self-adjoint coset mass-squared operator associated with an EIII vacuum. The
arithmetic input is the shifted divisor
\[
  m_\rho^2=-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2
\]
attached to the nontrivial zeros of the completed Riemann zeta function. The
coset spectral zeta sector is the assertion that this arithmetic divisor is
realized, equivariantly and with the mass-squared coordinate preserved, inside
the positive spectrum of that coset subalgebra.

This section makes that assertion precise and derives its consequence. The
finite adelic orbit data explain why rational primes supply the arithmetic
trace. The completed zeta factor supplies the archimedean normalization. The
coset spectral operator supplies positivity. Once these three pieces are
identified by a Galois-equivariant coset spectral realization, the shifted
zeta divisor is forced to lie in \(\mathbb R_{\ge0}\), which is equivalent to
the Riemann hypothesis.

Let \(\mathbb B=\{0,1\}\) and define
\[
  \mathcal E:\mathbb C\setminus\mathbb B\longrightarrow\mathbb C,
  \qquad
  \mathcal E(s)
  =
  \frac12\,\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2}\right)\zeta(s).
\]
Equivalently, if
\[
  \xi_{\mathrm R}(s)
  =
  \frac12\,s(s-1)\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2}\right)\zeta(s)
\]
denotes Riemann's completed entire zeta function
\cite{Riemann1859,Edwards1974,Titchmarsh1986}, then
\(\mathcal E(s)=\xi_{\mathrm R}(s)/(s(s-1))\). Set
\[
  \lambda=\left(s-\frac12\right)^2,
  \qquad
  \Phi(\lambda)
  =
  \mathcal E\left(\frac12+\sqrt{\lambda}\right).
\]
The functional equation gives
\(\mathcal E(\frac12+z)=\mathcal E(\frac12-z)\), so \(\Phi\) is independent of
the branch of \(\sqrt{\lambda}\).

The coordinate \(\lambda\) is the bridge to the coset sector. On the UCFT side
it is interpreted as the negative of a mass-squared eigenvalue. Thus the
question of whether zeros of \(\mathcal E\) lie on the critical line becomes
the question of whether the corresponding shifted mass-squared values are real
and nonnegative in the coset spectrum. The definitions below make
this spectral identification precise.

\begin{definition}[Finite Adelic Albert--EIII Completion]
\label{def:FiniteAdelic}
Let \(J_{\mathbb Z}\) be an integral form of the Albert algebra, the
\emph{integral Albert--EIII charge lattice}; for definiteness one may take the
lattice of Hermitian $3\times3$ matrices over the Coxeter order of integral
octonions. Its finite adelic completion is
\[
  J_{\mathbb A_f}
  =
  J_{\mathbb Z}\otimes_{\mathbb Z}\mathbb A_f .
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Zeta Sector]
\label{def:ZetaSector}
The \emph{zeta sector} \(\mathcal H_\zeta\subset\mathcal H\) is the closed
Hilbert subspace selected, under the coset correspondence, by
\(J_{\mathbb A_f}\) together with a one-dimensional automorphic character (the
\emph{zeta automorphic character}).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
  The sector is normalized so that its finite
  orbit space is
  \[
    \mathcal O_{\zeta}
    \cong
    \mathbb A_f^\times/\widehat{\mathbb Z}^{\,\times},
    \qquad
    \widehat{\mathbb Z}^{\,\times}
    =
    \prod_p \mathbb Z_p^\times ,
  \]
  the idele-class datum of Tate's thesis \cite{Tate1967}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Archimedean Zeta Oscillator]
The corresponding archimedean oscillator is
\[
  Z_\infty(s)
  =
  \pi^{-s/2}\Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2}\right).
\]
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Zeta-Sector Quadratic Operator]
\label{thm:ZetaQuadraticOperator}
Suppose the zeta-sector quadratic form obtained from the second variation of
the sectoral action is symmetric, closed, and semibounded. Then it defines a
self-adjoint mass-squared operator
\[
  M_\zeta^2:\operatorname{Dom}(M_\zeta^2)\subset\mathcal H_\zeta
  \longrightarrow\mathcal H_\zeta .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is the first representation theorem for closed, densely defined,
semibounded quadratic forms (Kato), which specializes to the Friedrichs
extension when the form is that of a semibounded symmetric operator.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Zeta Determinant Realization]
\label{def:ZetaDeterminant}
The zeta sector is said to realize the completed zeta determinant if there
exists a nonzero constant \(C\in\mathbb C^\times\) such that
\[
  \Phi(\lambda)
  =
  C\,\det_{\mathrm{reg}}\left(\lambda I+M_\zeta^2\right).
\]
\end{definition}

This is the spectral-determinant assumption in the sense of the trace-formula
programs of Connes and Berry--Keating
\cite{Connes1999,BerryKeating1999}, after passage to the shifted-square
coordinate \(\lambda=(s-\frac12)^2\).

\begin{lemma}[Prime-Orbit Correspondence]
\label{lem:PrimeOrbitCorrespondence}
Call a finite zeta orbit \emph{primitive} if it corresponds to an
indecomposable element of the positive cone of
\(\mathcal O_{\zeta}\cong\bigoplus_p\mathbb Z\). There is a canonical
bijection between primitive finite zeta orbits and rational primes.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Every finite idele \(a=(a_p)_p\in\mathbb A_f^\times\) has a unique
factorization \cite{Tate1967}
\[
  a_p=p^{v_p(a_p)}u_p,
  \qquad
  u_p\in\mathbb Z_p^\times,
\]
where \(v_p(a_p)\in\mathbb Z\) and \(v_p(a_p)=0\) for all but finitely many
primes \(p\). Hence
\[
  \mathbb A_f^\times
  \cong
  \left(\bigoplus_p p^{\mathbb Z}\right)
  \times
  \widehat{\mathbb Z}^{\,\times},
  \qquad
  \mathbb A_f^\times/\widehat{\mathbb Z}^{\,\times}
  \cong
  \bigoplus_p\mathbb Z.
\]
The positive cone \(\bigoplus_p\mathbb Z_{\ge0}\) is the free commutative monoid
of nonzero ideals of \(\mathbb Z\), and its indecomposable elements are the
prime ideals \((p)\). Primitive zeta orbits correspond to these
indecomposable elements.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Prime-Orbit Length]
\label{cor:PrimeOrbitLength}
Under the idele-norm character, a primitive finite zeta orbit associated with
the prime \(p\) has logarithmic length \(\log p\).
\end{corollary}

\begin{lemma}[Finite Arithmetic Trace]
\label{lem:FiniteArithmeticTrace}
Define the finite prime-orbit trace as the orbit sum, in the sense of the
trace-formula programs \cite{Connes1999},
\[
  \Tr_{\mathrm{fin}}(s)
  =
  -\sum_p\sum_{k\ge1}(\log p)\,e^{-ks\log p}
  =
  -\sum_p\sum_{k\ge1}(\log p)\,p^{-ks},
\]
the sum running over the primitive orbits of
\autoref{lem:PrimeOrbitCorrespondence} and their iterates, weighted by the
lengths of \autoref{cor:PrimeOrbitLength}. Then, for \(\Re(s)>1\),
\[
  \Tr_{\mathrm{fin}}(s)
  =
  \frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Equivalently,
\[
  \Tr_{\mathrm{fin}}(s)
  =
  -\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{\Lambda(n)}{n^s},
\]
where \(\Lambda\) is the von Mangoldt function
\cite{IwaniecKowalski2004}. The Euler product for \(\zeta\), valid for
\(\Re(s)>1\), gives the stated identity.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Completed zeta trace]
\label{lem:CompletedZetaTrace}
The logarithmic derivative of \(\mathcal E\) is
\[
  \frac{\mathcal E'(s)}{\mathcal E(s)}
  =
  -\frac12\log\pi
  +\frac12\psi\left(\frac{s}{2}\right)
  +\frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)},
  \qquad
  \psi(z)=\frac{\Gamma'(z)}{\Gamma(z)}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Take the logarithmic derivative of
\[
  \mathcal E(s)
  =
  \frac12\,\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2}\right)\zeta(s).
\]
\end{proof}

\noindent Putting \(s=\frac12+\sqrt{\lambda}\), \autoref{lem:CompletedZetaTrace} gives
\[
  \frac{\Phi'(\lambda)}{\Phi(\lambda)}
  =
  \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\lambda}}
  \left[
    -\frac12\log\pi
    +\frac12\psi\left(\frac14+\frac12\sqrt{\lambda}\right)
    +\frac{\zeta'(\frac12+\sqrt{\lambda})}
           {\zeta(\frac12+\sqrt{\lambda})}
  \right].
\]
For \(\Re(\frac12+\sqrt{\lambda})>1\), this becomes
\[
  \frac{\Phi'(\lambda)}{\Phi(\lambda)}
  =
  \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\lambda}}
  \left[
    -\frac12\log\pi
    +\frac12\psi\left(\frac14+\frac12\sqrt{\lambda}\right)
    -\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}
      \frac{\Lambda(n)}{n^{1/2+\sqrt{\lambda}}}
  \right].
\]

\begin{lemma}[Shifted-Square Equivalence]
\label{lem:ShiftedSquareEquivalence}
The Riemann hypothesis is equivalent to the assertion that every zero of
\(\Phi\) lies on \(\mathbb R_{\le0}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If the Riemann hypothesis holds, every zero of \(\mathcal E\) has the form
\[
  s=\frac12+i\gamma,
  \qquad \gamma\in\mathbb R.
\]
Thus
\[
  \lambda=\left(s-\frac12\right)^2=-\gamma^2\le0.
\]
Conversely, suppose every zero of \(\Phi\) lies on \(\mathbb R_{\le0}\). If
\(\rho\) is a zero of \(\mathcal E\), then
\[
  \lambda_\rho=\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2=-\gamma^2
\]
for some \(\gamma\in\mathbb R\). Hence
\(\rho=\frac12\pm i\gamma\), so \(\Re(\rho)=\frac12\). Since the zeros of
\(\mathcal E\) are precisely the nontrivial zeros of \(\zeta(s)\), the Riemann
hypothesis follows \cite{Titchmarsh1986}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann Hypothesis as Coset Positivity]
\label{thm:NoTachyonRH}
Assume the Albert--EIII postulates and suppose the zeta divisor is realized by the
positive UCFT coset spectral data in the sense of
\autoref{def:GaloisRealization}. Then the Riemann hypothesis holds.
Equivalently, the determinant has no tachyonic or complex mass-squared zeros:
all determinant zeros occur at
\[
  \lambda=-m_n^2,
  \qquad
  m_n^2\in\mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]
This statement is a conditional reduction, not an existence theorem: the
realization hypothesis is precisely the unconstructed Hilbert--P\'olya datum
(\autoref{rmk:HilbertPolya}), and nothing in this paper constructs it.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The UCFT coset spectral operator of \autoref{def:UCFTSpectralAlgebra} is
self-adjoint and nonnegative after gauge fixing and removal of zero modes.
Therefore its mass-squared spectrum lies in \(\mathbb R_{\ge0}\). A
mass-coordinate-preserving realization of the zeta divisor in this coset
spectrum forces every arithmetic value
\[
  m_\rho^2=-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2
\]
to be real and nonnegative. Thus every zero of \(\Phi\) lies on
\(\mathbb R_{\le0}\) in the shifted-square coordinate. By
\autoref{lem:ShiftedSquareEquivalence}, this is equivalent to the Riemann
hypothesis. In determinant language, the same statement says that zeros occur
only at \(\lambda=-m_n^2\) with \(m_n^2\ge0\), so no tachyonic or complex
mass-squared zeros occur.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Arithmetic Mass Divisor]
\label{def:ArithmeticMassDivisor}
Let \(\xi(s)\) denote the completed Riemann zeta function, and let
\[
Z_{\xi}:=\{\rho\in\mathbb C:\xi(\rho)=0,\ 0<\Re(\rho)<1\}
\]
be the nontrivial zero divisor, counted with multiplicity. Define the shifted
arithmetic mass-squared divisor
\[
\Sigma_{\zeta}
:=
\left\{
m_{\rho}^{2}:= -\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^{2}
\;:\;
\rho\in Z_{\xi}
\right\}.
\]
The functional equation identifies the pair \(\rho\) and \(1-\rho\), since
\[
-\left(1-\rho-\frac12\right)^2
=
-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2.
\]
Complex conjugation acts by
\[
m_{\rho}^{2}\longmapsto \overline{m_{\rho}^{2}}.
\]
We equip \(\Sigma_{\zeta}\) with its natural locally finite divisor topology.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[UCFT Spectral Algebra]
\label{def:UCFTSpectralAlgebra}
Let \(\phi_{0}\in \mathrm{EIII}\) be a vacuum and let
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}
=
\nabla^{*}\nabla+\mathcal R+\Hess_{\phi_{0}}(V)
\]
be the quadratic coset operator in UCFT, realized in the zeta sector
\(\mathcal H_\zeta\) of \autoref{def:ZetaSector}, so that
\(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}=M_\zeta^{2}\) in the notation of
\autoref{thm:ZetaQuadraticOperator}.
After the required gauge fixing and removal of zero modes, take
\(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\) to be a self-adjoint nonnegative operator on a
Hilbert space \(\mathcal H_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\subseteq\mathcal H_\zeta\). Let
\[
\Sigma_{\mathrm{UCFT}}
:=
\operatorname{Spec}(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}})
\subseteq \mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]
Define the commutative spectral \(C^{*}\)-algebra
\[
\mathcal A_{\mathrm{UCFT}}
:=
C_{0}(\Sigma_{\mathrm{UCFT}}).
\]
Similarly define
\[
\mathcal A_{\zeta}
:=
C_{0}(\Sigma_{\zeta}),
\]
where \(\Sigma_{\zeta}\) is the arithmetic mass-squared divisor.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Status of the Realization Hypothesis]
\label{rmk:RealizationStatus}
The realization posited below is a hypothesis, not a construction. It is a
statement about the sectoral operator \(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}=M_\zeta^{2}\)
of the adelically selected zeta sector, not about the Laplace-type operator of
the compact $\sigma$-model sector, whose spectrum is fixed by harmonic
analysis on EIII and is manifestly not the arithmetic divisor. Nothing in
this paper constructs a zeta sector whose spectrum is \(\Sigma_\zeta\);
the theorems below assert only the implication from its existence.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Galois-Equivariant Spectral Realization]
\label{def:GaloisRealization}
A Galois-equivariant spectral realization of the zeta data in the UCFT coset
sector is a \(*\)-isomorphism of commutative spectral \(C^{*}\)-algebras
\[
\Theta:\mathcal A_{\zeta}\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathcal A_{\mathrm{UCFT}}
\]
such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \(\Theta\) preserves the distinguished mass-squared coordinate;
\item \(\Theta\) intertwines complex conjugation on \(\Sigma_{\zeta}\) with the
real structure of the UCFT Hilbert-space representation;
\item at every finite algebraic approximation level, \(\Theta\) is compatible
with the relevant Galois action on the arithmetic mass divisor and the induced
algebraic symmetry action on the UCFT spectral data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the Hilbert--P\'olya Conjecture]
\label{rmk:HilbertPolya}
The construction above is a coset-sector instance of the Hilbert--P\'olya
conjecture: the proposal that the ordinates \(\gamma\) of the nontrivial
zeros \(\rho=\frac12+i\gamma\) are the spectrum of a self-adjoint operator,
so that self-adjointness alone forces \(\gamma\in\mathbb R\) and hence the
Riemann hypothesis \cite{BerryKeatingSIAM1999,Connes1999}. In our language,
self-adjointness of \(\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\) follows from the semibounded
closed-form construction (\autoref{thm:ZetaQuadraticOperator}), and its
nonnegativity is imposed as structural input
(\autoref{def:UCFTSpectralAlgebra}); neither is the hard part. The hard part is
the arithmetic realization.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Coset Spectral Criterion for the Riemann Hypothesis]
\label{thm:CosetSpectralCriterion}
Assume the Albert--EIII postulates. If there exists a Galois-equivariant spectral realization
\[
\Theta:\mathcal A_{\zeta}\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathcal A_{\mathrm{UCFT}},
\]
then the Riemann hypothesis holds. This is a conditional reduction in the
Hilbert--P\'olya sense: the existence of \(\Theta\) is the open hypothesis,
and the theorem does not assert it.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since
\[
\Theta:\mathcal A_{\zeta}\xrightarrow{\sim}\mathcal A_{\mathrm{UCFT}}
\]
is a \(*\)-isomorphism of commutative \(C^{*}\)-algebras, Gelfand duality gives
a homeomorphism of spectra
\[
\Theta^{\vee}:\Sigma_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\xrightarrow{\sim}\Sigma_{\zeta}.
\]
The UCFT coset spectral operator is self-adjoint and nonnegative, hence
\[
\Sigma_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\subseteq\mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]
Because the realization preserves the distinguished mass-squared coordinate,
the transported arithmetic mass-squared divisor also satisfies
\[
\Sigma_{\zeta}\subseteq\mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]

Let \(\rho\in Z_{\xi}\). Write
\[
\rho=\frac12+\alpha+i\gamma,
\qquad
\alpha,\gamma\in\mathbb R.
\]
Then
\[
m_{\rho}^{2}
=
-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^{2}
=
-(\alpha+i\gamma)^{2}
=
\gamma^{2}-\alpha^{2}-2i\alpha\gamma.
\]
Since \(m_{\rho}^{2}\in\mathbb R_{\ge0}\), its imaginary part vanishes:
\[
-2\alpha\gamma=0.
\]
For a nontrivial zero of the completed Riemann zeta function in the critical
strip, \(\gamma\neq0\). Hence
\[
\alpha=0.
\]
Thus
\[
\Re(\rho)=\frac12.
\]
Since this holds for every nontrivial zero \(\rho\), the Riemann hypothesis
follows.
\end{proof}

The criterion of \autoref{thm:CosetSpectralCriterion} is rigid at its endpoints:
it demands a single $*$-isomorphism identifying the arithmetic divisor with
the coset spectrum exactly. For approximation arguments---in particular for
any attempt to build a realization level by level from the finite adelic data
of \autoref{def:ZetaSector}---a deformation-adapted formulation
is preferable. We therefore replace the single isomorphism by a
continuous family of operators interpolating between the coset endpoint and
the arithmetic endpoint.

\begin{definition}[Locally Finite Divisor Topology]
Let \(\operatorname{Div}_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathbb C)\) denote the space of
locally finite effective divisors on \(\mathbb C\). Thus an element is a formal
sum
\[
D=\sum_{\lambda\in\mathbb C}m_{\lambda}[\lambda],
\qquad
m_{\lambda}\in\mathbb Z_{\ge0},
\]
such that only finitely many \(\lambda\)'s occur in each compact subset of
\(\mathbb C\).

We equip \(\operatorname{Div}_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathbb C)\) with the vague
topology: a sequence \(D_n\) converges to \(D\) if and only if, for every
compactly supported continuous test function \(\varphi\in C_c(\mathbb C)\),
\[
\sum_{\lambda}m_{\lambda}^{(n)}\varphi(\lambda)
\longrightarrow
\sum_{\lambda}m_{\lambda}\varphi(\lambda).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Spectral Continuity]
Let \(B\) be a parameter space containing a coset point \(b_{\mathrm{UCFT}}\)
and an arithmetic point \(b_{\zeta}\). A spectral bridge for the coset
correspondence is a family of closed operators
\[
\{\mathcal M_b^2\}_{b\in B}
\]
such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item each \(\mathcal M_b^2\) acts on a Hilbert space \(\mathcal H_b\), and
the \(\mathcal H_b\) are unitarily identified with a fixed Hilbert space
(or form a continuous field of Hilbert spaces over \(B\)), so that the
resolvent comparison in the next item is meaningful;
\item the family is continuous in the norm-resolvent topology, meaning that
for some, equivalently every, \(w\in\mathbb C\setminus\mathbb R\),
\[
b\longmapsto (\mathcal M_b^2-w)^{-1}
\]
is norm-continuous;
\item each \(\mathcal M_b^2\) has compact resolvent and zeta-regularizable
spectrum, and hence admits a regularized determinant in the
mass-squared coordinate,
\[
D_b(\mu):=\det_{\mathrm{reg}}(\mathcal M_b^2-\mu),
\]
whose zero divisor \(\operatorname{div}(D_b)\) is the eigenvalue divisor of
\(\mathcal M_b^2\), counted with multiplicity;
\item the zero-divisor map
\[
b\longmapsto \operatorname{div}(D_b)
\]
is continuous as a map
\[
B\longrightarrow \operatorname{Div}_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathbb C).
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Galois-Compatible Spectral Bridge]
A spectral bridge is Galois-compatible if, at every finite algebraic
approximation level, the divisor
\[
\operatorname{div}(D_b)
\]
is stable under the relevant Galois action, and the transition maps in the
bridge intertwine this Galois action with the real structure on the quantum
Hilbert spaces \(\mathcal H_b\).

Equivalently, for every admissible finite approximation \(D_{b,N}\), every
Galois element \(\sigma\), and every point \(\lambda\) of the divisor, one has
\[
\sigma(\lambda)\in \operatorname{supp}(D_{b,N})
\]
with the same multiplicity, and this compatibility is preserved under passage
to the limiting divisor.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Positive Continuous Albert--Zeta Realization]
\label{def:PositiveRealization}
A \emph{Positive Continuous Albert--Zeta Realization} is a Galois-compatible
spectral bridge
\[
\{\mathcal M_b^2\}_{b\in B}
\]
such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the UCFT endpoint satisfies
\[
\mathcal M_{b_{\mathrm{UCFT}}}^{2}
=
\Delta_{\mathrm{UCFT}};
\]
\item the arithmetic endpoint satisfies
\[
\operatorname{div}(D_{b_{\zeta}})
=
\Sigma_{\zeta}
=
\left\{
-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2:
\xi(\rho)=0,\ 0<\Re(\rho)<1
\right\};
\]
\item every operator in the bridge is self-adjoint and nonnegative:
\[
\mathcal M_b^2=\left(\mathcal M_b^2\right)^{*},
\qquad
\mathcal M_b^2\ge0
\]
for all \(b\in B\).
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous Coset Criterion for the Riemann Hypothesis]
\label{thm:ContinuousCosetCriterion}
Assume the Albert--EIII postulates. If a Positive Continuous Albert--Zeta
Realization exists, then the Riemann hypothesis holds.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For each \(b\in B\), self-adjointness implies
\[
\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal M_b^2)\subseteq\mathbb R.
\]
Nonnegativity strengthens this to
\[
\operatorname{Spec}(\mathcal M_b^2)\subseteq\mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]
Since
\[
D_b(\mu)=\det_{\mathrm{reg}}(\mathcal M_b^2-\mu),
\]
the zero divisor of \(D_b\) lies in the mass-squared spectrum:
\[
\operatorname{div}(D_b)\subseteq\mathbb R_{\ge0}.
\]

At the arithmetic endpoint \(b=b_{\zeta}\), the zero divisor is
\[
\Sigma_{\zeta}
=
\left\{
-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2:
\xi(\rho)=0,\ 0<\Re(\rho)<1
\right\}.
\]
Thus every shifted arithmetic mass-squared value is real and nonnegative.

Let
\[
\rho=\frac12+\alpha+i\gamma,
\qquad
\alpha,\gamma\in\mathbb R.
\]
Then
\[
-\left(\rho-\frac12\right)^2
=
-(\alpha+i\gamma)^2
=
\gamma^2-\alpha^2-2i\alpha\gamma.
\]
Since this number is real, its imaginary part vanishes:
\[
-2\alpha\gamma=0.
\]
A nontrivial zero of \(\xi(s)\) in the critical strip is not real, so
\[
\gamma\neq0.
\]
Hence
\[
\alpha=0.
\]
Therefore
\[
\Re(\rho)=\frac12.
\]
Since this holds for every nontrivial zero, the Riemann hypothesis follows.
\end{proof}

\section{Heterotic Correspondence and the Albert--EIII Swampland Criterion}
\label{sec:StringTheory}
%======================================================================%

The construction developed above realizes gravity and spacetime as
induced structures in the visible coset sector: no independent graviton is
postulated there, and the metric
$g_{\mu\nu}$ is the pull-back of the EIII metric along a spacetime-filling
configuration (\autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}), while the Planck mass is
generated radiatively in the sense of Sakharov and Adler
\cite{SakharovInducedGravity,AdlerInducedGravity}. This motivates a precise,
but limited, comparison between the coset sector and the massless bosonic
sector of an $\E_{8}\times E_{8}$ heterotic compactification \cite{GHMR:1985}
whose visible gauge group is $\ESix$. We emphasize at the outset that the
statement established here is local, two-derivative, and current-algebraic. The
purpose of this section is to formulate the heterotic correspondence, identify a
concrete $\mathbb Z_{12}$ heterotic candidate, and state the resulting
Albert--EIII swampland criterion.

Fix an $\E_{8}\times E_{8}$ heterotic background on
$\mathbb R^{1,3}\times X$ with $X$ a six-dimensional internal space whose
worldsheet realization preserves an $\ESix$ current algebra at level one, so
that the visible massless spectrum organizes into $\ESix$ multiplets and the
$\mathbf{78}$ adjoint is present as a gauge multiplet. Let $M_{\mathrm{res}}$ denote the
residual scalar target obtained after the internal CFT has fixed all moduli
except those of the visible $\ESix$-breaking sector.  We write $H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$
for the grand-unified isotropy group singled out by an adjoint Higgs vacuum in
the $\ESix\supset H$ singlet direction (\autoref{thm:Z12Orbit}).

\begin{definition}[Heterotic Correspondence Map]
\label{def:Interface}
The \emph{heterotic correspondence map} is the $\ESix$-equivariant map
\[
  F\ :\ M_{\mathrm{res}}\;\longrightarrow\;\M=\MCosetInline
\]
sending the gauge-unfixed adjoint-Higgs orbit of the heterotic model onto
$\M$, and identifying the residual scalar fluctuations with the
Goldstone coordinates $\phi^{\alpha}$ of \autoref{sec:EmergentFields}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Two-Derivative Matching]
\label{lem:TwoDerivative}
On the image of $F$ the heterotic non-linear sigma-model metric restricted to the
visible $\ESix$-breaking directions coincides, up to an overall normalization
$f^{2}$, with the pull-back $F^{\ast}\mathfrak g$ of the unique
$\ESix$-invariant EIII metric $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}=f^{2}(-K_{\alpha\beta})$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The residual visible directions transform in
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$
under $H$, which is real-irreducible (\autoref{lem:Irrep}).  Any
$H$-invariant symmetric two-tensor on this representation is unique up to scale
by Schur's lemma; both the sigma-model metric and $\mathfrak g$ are such
tensors, hence proportional.  The proportionality constant defines
$f^{2}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Current-Algebra Matching]
\label{lem:Current}
The $H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$ isometry action on $\M$ is generated by the same
currents as the level-one $\SOTen$ and $\UOne_\psi$ Ka\v{c}--Moody currents of
the heterotic worldsheet theory, with matched central data.  In particular the
$\UOne_\psi$ charge assignment
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$ reproduces the worldsheet
charge lattice of the visible matter.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The Killing vector fields of $\M$ generating $H$ act on the tangent
representation exactly as the zero modes of the $\SOTen\times\UOne_\psi$
currents act on the visible states; the charges agree by construction of $F$,
and the $\UOne_\psi$ normalization is fixed by requiring the $\mathbf{16}$ to carry
charge $-3$, matching the branching
$\mathbf{78}=\mathbf{45}_{0}\oplus\mathbf{1}_{0}
\oplus\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Local Heterotic Realization]
\label{thm:Holographic}
Assume the heterotic correspondence map \(F\) of \autoref{def:Interface} exists for a
heterotic background satisfying the stated current-algebra data. Then, in a
neighbourhood of the gauge-unfixed Higgs orbit, the two-derivative bosonic
action of the visible $\ESix$-breaking sector of the heterotic model is mapped by
$F$ onto the coset action of \autoref{sec:EmergentFields}, with the induced
Einstein--Hilbert term of \autoref{sec:sigma} providing the gravitational
sector.  Under this map the Green--Schwarz axion \cite{GreenSchwarz:1984} of the
heterotic background is identified with the $\UOne_\psi$ circle-fiber (phase)
mode of $S_{v}$---the Goldstone absorbed by the St\"uckelberg mechanism
(\autoref{cor:VacuumManifold})---rather than with a coordinate on $\M$ itself.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Combine \autoref{lem:TwoDerivative} and \autoref{lem:Current}: the kinetic term
matches by two-derivative matching and the symmetry action matches by
current-algebra matching, so the visible bosonic Lagrangians agree to two
derivatives.  The gravitational sector is not present in the heterotic
two-derivative visible action as an independent field; on the coset side it is
induced (\autoref{sec:sigma}), consistent with the absence of a fundamental
visible-sector graviton. The Green--Schwarz axion couples to
$\Tr F\wedge F$ with a shift symmetry, and the correspondence identifies the
corresponding visible shift mode with the $\UOne_\psi$-charged circle-fiber
phase of $S_v$, or with the universal heterotic axion matched to that same
anomalous shift, rather than with a coordinate on $\M$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the Correspondence]
\label{rmk:HolographicScope}
\autoref{thm:Holographic} is a statement about the two-derivative bosonic action
and the current algebra only. It does not assert an exact conformal duality, a
matching of the full string spectrum, or agreement of higher-derivative
$\alpha'$ corrections. In particular the heterotic side carries an infinite
tower of massive string states with no coset counterpart, and the coset side is an
effective field theory valid below the string scale. The value of the
correspondence is structural: it exhibits the coset as the low-energy image
of a genuine string background, and it shows that the induced-gravity mechanism
is compatible with a formulation in which no fundamental visible-sector
graviton is postulated.
\end{remark}

We now consider a heterotic compactification intended to meet the criteria of
\autoref{thm:Holographic}: a $\mathbb Z_{12}$ asymmetric orbifold of the
$\E_{8}\times E_{8}$ heterotic string \cite{DHVW:1985,Narain:1986} whose visible
sector is a three-generation $\ESix$ grand unified theory with an adjoint Higgs
$\mathbf{78}_{H}$ \cite{Z12Heterotic}. Giving the adjoint Higgs a vacuum expectation
value in the $\ESix\supset H$ singlet direction breaks $\ESix\to H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$,
and the local orbit computation identifies the resulting gauge-unfixed Higgs
orbit with the coset $\M$.

The model is a $\mathbb Z_{12}$ asymmetric orbifold with visible gauge group
$\ESix$ realized at level one, three chiral generations in the $\mathbf{27}$, and an
adjoint Higgs multiplet $\mathbf{78}_{H}$ arising from the diagonal embedding of the
visible $\ESix$ in the ten-dimensional gauge group.  Under
$\ESix\supset H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$ the adjoint branches as
\begin{equation}
  \mathbf{78}
  \;=\;
  \mathbf{45}_{0}\,\oplus\,\mathbf{1}_{0}\,\oplus\,
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\,\oplus\,\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3},
  \label{eq:AdjointBranch}
\end{equation}
and we take the Higgs vacuum $\langle\mathbf{78}_{H}\rangle=\Phi_{0}$ to lie in
the $\mathbf{1}_{0}$ direction.

\begin{theorem}[Local Heterotic Realization of the Coset]
\label{thm:Z12Orbit}
Assume the $\mathbb Z_{12}$ model contains an adjoint-Higgs vacuum
\(\Phi_{0}\) in the $\mathbf{1}_{0}\subset\mathbf{78}$ direction under
$\ESix\supset H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$.  Then the gauge-unfixed Higgs orbit is
\[
  \mathcal O_{H}
  \;=\;\ESix\cdot\Phi_{0}
  \;\cong\;\MCosetInline
  \;=\;\M,
  \qquad
  T_{\mathbb C}\,\mathcal O_{H}
  =
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the branching \eqref{eq:AdjointBranch} the centralizer in $\ESix$ of a
generic vector in the $\mathbf{1}_{0}$ direction is exactly $H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$: the
$\mathbf{45}_{0}$ commutes with $\Phi_{0}$, the $\UOne_\psi$ generator is $\Phi_{0}$
itself, and the $\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$ carry non-zero $\UOne_\psi$
charge and are therefore rotated.  By the orbit--stabilizer theorem
$\ESix\cdot\Phi_{0}\cong\ESix/H=\MCosetInline$.  The tangent space at $\Phi_{0}$
is the span of the broken generators, namely
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$, giving the stated complexified tangent
representation.  This is the coset $\M$ of \autoref{sec:EmergentFields}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Gauge-Fixed/Gauge-Unfixed Equivalence]
\label{thm:Z12Equivalence}
The EIII scalar sector is physically equivalent to the gauge-unfixed
Stueckelberg/Higgs-orbit description of the $\ESix\to\SOTenXUOne_\psi$ breaking
sector of the $\mathbb Z_{12}$ model.  Unitary gauge maps this description to the
standard massive-vector description of the heterotic $\ESix$ grand unified
theory, in which the $\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$ Goldstones are absorbed by
the broken gauge bosons.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
In the gauge-unfixed description the $32$ real coordinates of
$\mathcal O_{H}\cong\M$ are Stueckelberg/Goldstone fields for the broken
$\ESix/H$ directions.  Fixing unitary gauge sets the Goldstones to zero and gives
mass to the corresponding gauge bosons via the Higgs mechanism; the massive
vector content is gauge-invariant and hence the same in both descriptions. The
coset action of \autoref{sec:EmergentFields} is the gauge-unfixed
Goldstone dynamics on $\mathcal O_{H}$, so the two descriptions are equivalent
at the level of this sector.
\end{proof}

with the first step realized geometrically as the emergence of the coset

\begin{corollary}[Local Heterotic Realization of the Coset]
\label{cor:UVComplete}
If the $\mathbb Z_{12}$ model of \cite{Z12Heterotic} satisfies the global

\begin{proof}
Immediate from \autoref{thm:Z12Orbit}, \autoref{thm:Holographic}, and the

The results above give a relative swampland criterion. We do not claim

the data specified below.

\begin{theorem}[Albert--EIII Swampland Criterion]
\label{thm:Swampland}
Let $\mathcal T$ be a four-dimensional effective theory proposed as the
Albert-selected low-energy sector of an $\E_{8}\times E_{8}$ heterotic
compactification.  Assume that its visible exceptional sector is selected by the
Albert algebra $\Albert$ and that the corresponding compact real form is $\ESix$.
Then $\mathcal T$ lies in the Albert-selected heterotic landscape only if its
gauge-unfixed Higgs/moduli sector contains a residual scalar orbit
\[
  \mathcal O\;\cong\;\MCosetInline\;=\;\M,
  \qquad
  T_{\mathbb C}\,\mathcal O
  =
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3},
\]
carrying the unique positive $\ESix$-invariant EIII metric, globally consistent
$\SOTenXUOne_\psi$ gauge data, anomaly cancellation, spin-lift compatibility,
Green--Schwarz matching, and heterotic worldsheet consistency.  Equivalently, any
proposed Albert-selected four-dimensional effective theory failing one of these
conditions lies outside the Albert--EIII landscape: it has no
$\E_{8}\times E_{8}$ heterotic ultraviolet completion of the specified type
realizing the coset correspondence.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument is by necessity together with the contrapositive.

Albert selection fixes the complex exceptional type
to $\ESix$ (\autoref{sec:PostulatesAlbertSSB}); choosing the compact real form gives the
compact $\ESix$ structure used in the EIII target and guarantees a
positive-definite invariant metric (\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}).

Imposing the grand-unified isotropy $H=\SOTenXUOne_\psi$ forces the homogeneous
scalar orbit to be $\ESix/H$. By the orbit--stabilizer theorem an adjoint Higgs
vacuum in the $\mathbf{1}_{0}$ direction of the branching \eqref{eq:AdjointBranch}
has centralizer $H$, so its gauge-unfixed Higgs orbit is exactly
$\ESix\cdot\Phi_{0}\cong\MCosetInline$
(\autoref{thm:Z12Orbit}).

The tangent representation is forced to be
$\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}$, which is real-irreducible. Hence the
$H$-invariant positive metric is unique up to scale, and there is no competing
compact homogeneous residual scalar sector with the same Albert/$\ESix$/$H$
data (\autoref{lem:Irrep}, \autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}).

Because $\mathfrak m$ has no invariant four-plane and the EIII metric is positive
definite, the coset cannot supply a Lorentzian vierbein by selecting four invariant
scalar directions. The valid construction is the nondegenerate Euclidean pull-back of
\autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}, followed by Osterwalder--Schrader
reconstruction; gravity then enters through the induced Sakharov--Adler
dynamics of \autoref{sec:sigma}.

Local EIII matching is not sufficient; a heterotic
completion must further satisfy modular invariance, GSO/fermion-parity
consistency, flux and Wilson-line quantization, bundle transition-function
compatibility, anomaly cancellation on overlaps, spin-lift conditions for the
$\mathbf{16}$ matter, and Green--Schwarz matching \cite{GreenSchwarz:1984}.

If a proposed effective theory lacks the EIII orbit, the required tangent
representation, the EIII metric, the correct gauge action, the
anomaly/Green--Schwarz compatibility, the spin-lift data, or global heterotic
consistency, then it cannot be the Albert-selected $\E_{8}\times E_{8}$
ultraviolet completion of the specified type.
\end{proof}

\autoref{thm:Swampland}, and no other presently identified construction is known

\end{proof}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:Discussion}
%======================================================================%

The passage from the coset sector to observable physics is constrained first by
anomaly cancellation. This is already a point at which the coset truncation is
not, by itself, a complete four-dimensional theory. The low-energy field content
in \autoref{tab:FieldContent} gauges
\[
  H=\SOTen\cdot \UOne_{\psi}
\]
with the global quotient convention fixed in \autoref{rmk:RealForms}, and
contains chiral matter in a single \(\mathbf{16}_{+1}\). Although the
\(\SOTen\) factor is anomaly-free in four dimensions, the abelian factor is
not. A lone \(\mathbf{16}_{+1}\) leaves mixed and abelian anomalies of types
\[
  [\SOTen]^2\,\UOne_\psi,\qquad
  [\UOne_\psi]^3,\qquad
  \mathrm{grav}^{2}\,\UOne_\psi .
\]
These anomalies cancel identically for a complete
\[
  \mathbf{27}
  =
  \mathbf{16}_{+1}
  \oplus
  \mathbf{10}_{-2}
  \oplus
  \mathbf{1}_{+4}.
\]
Thus the phenomenological interpretation of the truncation must be made through
the heterotic boundary, where anomaly cancellation is supplied by additional
ultraviolet data.

There are two compatible ways this can occur. The first is a four-dimensional
Green--Schwarz mechanism \cite{GreenSchwarz:1984,DineSeibergWitten:1987}. In
the unprojectivized rank-one orbit
\[
  S_v\simeq \ESix/\Spin(10),
\]
there is a circle-fiber phase over
\[
  \M=\ESix/(\Spin(10)\cdot\UOne_\psi).
\]
This phase is not one of the \(32\) Goldstone coordinates of the projective
EIII base \(\M\); rather, it is the \(\UOne_\psi\)-charged fiber direction
removed in passing from \(S_v\) to \(\M\). Under the heterotic matching of
\autoref{thm:Holographic}, this circle-fiber phase may be matched to the
four-dimensional Green--Schwarz axion, or else supplemented by the universal
heterotic axion with the same anomalous shift. Its induced couplings to
\(\Tr(F\wedge F)\) shift under \(\UOne_\psi\) and cancel the residual mixed,
abelian, and gravitational anomalies. The same mechanism gives a Stueckelberg
mass, of order \(g f\) up to threshold normalization, to the \(\UOne_\psi\)
gauge boson. Below this scale the effective gauge group is the anomaly-free
\(\SOTen\), and the breaking chain of \autoref{cor:BreakingChain} proceeds in
the usual grand-unified manner.

The second possibility is spectrum completion. In the \(\mathbb Z_{12}\)
realization of \cite{Z12Heterotic}, the chiral matter appears in complete
\(\mathbf{27}\) generations. The four-dimensional chiral spectrum is then
anomaly-free at the level of complete \(\ESix\)-multiplets, while the
Green--Schwarz mechanism remains part of the universal heterotic anomaly
descent. In this sense, the coset truncation fixes the representation-theoretic
seed of one family, whereas family replication and complete anomaly
cancellation are properties of the ultraviolet completion.

The resulting phenomenology is therefore that of an \(\SOTen\) grand unified
theory subject to heterotic matching. The matching scale obtained in
\autoref{sec:FRG} is
\[
  M_{\mathrm{GUT}}\simeq 2\times 10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV},
\]
with the qualification that this scale is a consistency output of the
heterotic handoff, not a prediction of the nonsupersymmetric coset flow alone.
Dimension-six gauge-boson exchange then gives proton-decay channels with
partial lifetimes parametrically in the range
\[
  10^{35}\text{--}10^{37}\,\mathrm{yr},
\]
with the lower part of this interval within the projected reach of
next-generation searches \cite{NathFileviezPerez:2007}. The precise lifetime
depends on the threshold spectrum at the matching scale, the normalization of
the unified coupling, the heavy gauge-boson masses, and the hadronic matrix
elements entering the decay amplitudes. The construction should therefore be
read as restricting a proton-decay window, rather than predicting a single
lifetime.

Neutrino physics provides a second standard phenomenological channel. Each
\(\mathbf{16}\) contains a right-handed neutrino, so a seesaw mechanism becomes
available after \(\SOTen\) breaking. The Majorana scale is not fixed by the
Albert--EIII kinematics; it depends on the ultraviolet completion and on the
operators responsible for the descent from \(\SOTen\) to the Standard Model
gauge group. Nevertheless, the embedding constrains the allowed charge
assignments and places the neutrino sector in the same unified multiplet
structure as the charged matter fields.

The heavy states associated with the first breaking step are not expected to be
directly accessible at low energy. The \(Z'_{\psi}\) associated with the
Stueckelberg-massive \(\UOne_\psi\) lies near the matching scale. Likewise, the
\(28\) transverse coset scalars of \autoref{thm:Mass28}, located at the top of
the hierarchy described in \autoref{sec:sigma}, are structural features of the
ultraviolet matching rather than ordinary TeV-scale scalar states. Their
degeneracy is a consequence of the \(H\)-irreducibility of the EIII isotropy
representation, conditional on the stabilizing one-loop hypothesis used in
\autoref{thm:Mass28}; their precise mass scale is therefore part of the
effective matching data rather than an independently measurable low-energy
prediction.

The main conclusion is that UCFT should not be read as a complete autonomous
four-dimensional model with all low-energy data fixed by the coset alone. The
unconditional part of the construction is the exceptional representation theory:
the compact \(\ESix\) action on \(\AlbertC\), the EIII vacuum orbit, the
\(\mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}\) isotropy representation,
and the unique positive invariant coset metric. The conditional part begins
when this representation-theoretic data is promoted to a quantum field theory:
Osterwalder--Schrader reconstruction, induced gravity, anomaly cancellation,
heterotic matching, and the zeta spectral realization all require additional
hypotheses. Under those hypotheses, the coset sector supplies a rigid algebraic
skeleton for four-dimensional geometry, grand-unified matter, and arithmetic
spectral constraints; without them, it remains an effective Albert--EIII
truncation awaiting ultraviolet completion.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:Conclusion}
%======================================================================%

We have formulated Universal Coset Field Theory as a conditional
operator-algebraic framework organized by the compact EIII geometry of type
\(\ESix\) and by the complexified Albert algebra \(\AlbertC\). The central
unconditional input is representation-theoretic: the compact real form
\(\ESix\) acts on the minimal \(\mathbf{27}_{\mathbb C}\), the closed
projective orbit of a rank-one line is
\[
  \M=\ESix/(\Spin(10)\cdot\UOne),
\]
and the corresponding isotropy representation has complexification
\[
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3}.
\]
Consequently the coset has real dimension \(32\), carries a unique positive
\(\ESix\)-invariant metric up to scale, and supplies a rigid exceptional
geometric arena for four-dimensional field theory.

Within this arena, a spacetime-filling coset configuration induces a
four-dimensional Riemannian metric by pull-back. Thus the metric is not an
independent datum of the coset sector; it is the first fundamental form of the
map from the four-dimensional parameter manifold into EIII. The Lorentzian
theory is obtained only after imposing the usual Osterwalder--Schrader
conditions on the Euclidean Schwinger functions and reconstructing the
corresponding Hilbert-space representation. In this sense, UCFT treats
spacetime geometry as emergent from the coset representation of the local
observable algebra, while keeping the analytic reconstruction problem explicit.

The one-loop effective action then realizes the Sakharov--Adler mechanism:
heat-kernel coefficients generate an Einstein--Hilbert term in the effective
action. The invariant statement is the radiative origin of this term. Its
renormalized coefficient, and hence the precise value of the induced Planck
scale, remains dependent on the regulator, spectrum, and ultraviolet matching
data. Similarly, the degeneracy of the transverse coset scalars follows from
the \(H\)-irreducibility of the EIII isotropy representation, while their
positive mass scale depends on the stabilizing one-loop hypothesis and the
chosen normalization of the effective truncation.

The renormalization-group analysis supports the interpretation of the
four-dimensional coset model as an effective theory below its matching
boundary, rather than as a complete autonomous ultraviolet fixed point. The
heterotic sector is therefore not an optional phenomenological embellishment,
but the place where anomaly cancellation, family completion, threshold data,
and global consistency conditions enter. In particular, the single
\(\mathbf{16}_{+1}\) coset truncation is not by itself an anomaly-free gauged
\(\SOTen\cdot\UOne_\psi\) theory. Completion to full \(\mathbf{27}\)
multiplets, or a Green--Schwarz/Stueckelberg mechanism matched to the
heterotic axionic sector, is required for a consistent four-dimensional
interpretation.

The arithmetic sector has an analogous logical status. The zeta construction
does not give an unconditional proof of the Riemann hypothesis. Rather, it
identifies a precise conditional Hilbert--Pólya criterion internal to the
positive spectral algebra of the theory: if the shifted zeta divisor is
realized by a positive self-adjoint UCFT spectral operator in the prescribed
Galois-equivariant way, then all nontrivial zeros of the completed zeta
function lie on the critical line. The mathematical content is therefore a
conditional spectral reduction, not an unconstructed claim of existence.

The resulting picture is a rigid but explicitly conditional framework. The
Albert--EIII data determine the exceptional coset geometry, the
\(\Spin(10)\cdot\UOne\) branching, the \(32\)-dimensional Goldstone sector,
and the unique positive invariant metric. Quantum reconstruction, induced
gravity, anomaly cancellation, heterotic completion, and arithmetic spectral
realization require additional hypotheses. Under those hypotheses, UCFT
provides a unified algebraic setting in which four-dimensional geometry,
grand-unified matter structure, and zeta-spectral constraints arise from one
exceptional coset representation. Without those hypotheses, the construction
should be read more modestly as an effective Albert--EIII truncation whose
ultraviolet completion and spectral realization remain explicit matching
problems.

\section{Emergent Spacetime Proofs}
\label{app:Lorentz}
%======================================================================%

This appendix supplies proofs for \autoref{lem:Rank4} and \autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian} from \autoref{sec:EmergentFields}.

Recall that the broken directions of the coset
\[
    \M = \MCoset
\]
form a $32$-dimensional real vector space
\(\mathfrak m\) carrying the unique invariant metric
\(\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}=f^{2}(-K_{\alpha\beta})\) of
\autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}, which on the compact real form of \(\ESix\) is
positive definite. Let
\[
  \mathrm d\phi_x:
  T_x\, \mathbb R^{4}\to T_{\phi(x)}\, \mathcal M
  \cong\mathfrak m
\]
denote the differential of the map
\(\phi^\alpha(x)\).

\begin{proof}[Proof of \autoref{lem:Rank4}]
The pull-back at \(x\) is the quadratic form
\[
  g_{\mu\nu}(x)
  =\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}\,\partial_\mu\phi^{\alpha}\partial_\nu\phi^{\beta}
  =\mathfrak g\bigl(\mathrm d\phi_x(\partial_\mu),\,
          \mathrm d\phi_x(\partial_\nu)\bigr).
\]
Because \(\mathfrak g\) is positive definite, the associated Gram map obeys
\(\operatorname{rank} g_{\mu\nu}=\operatorname{rank}\mathrm d\phi_x=\dim
V_x\), where \(V_x=\Im\,\mathrm d\phi_x\subseteq\mathfrak m\): indeed
\(g_{\mu\nu}v^{\mu}=0\) iff \(\mathfrak g(\mathrm d\phi_x v,\,\cdot\,)=0\)
iff \(\mathrm d\phi_x v=0\) by definiteness. Since
\(\dim T_x\mathbb R^{4}=4\) we have \(\dim V_x\le4\), and non-degeneracy of
\(g_{\mu\nu}\) forces \(\dim V_x=4\), i.e.\ \(\mathrm d\phi_x\) is injective and
\(V_x=\mathfrak p_{0}(x)\) is a four-plane. Its \(\mathfrak g\)-orthogonal
complement \(\mathfrak p_{m}(x)\) has dimension \(28\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of \autoref{thm:EmergentRiemannian}]
Embed \(\SOTenXUOne\) in \(\ESix\) as the stabilizer of the rank-one
highest-weight line \([X_{0}]\in\mathbb P(\AlbertC)\)
(\autoref{cor:Isotropy}, \autoref{thm:OrbitClass}), and write
\(\mathfrak e_{6}=\mathfrak h\oplus\mathfrak m\) with
\(\mathfrak h=\mathfrak{so}(10)\oplus\mathfrak u(1)\). On the compact
real form of \(\ESix\) the Killing form is negative definite on all of
\(\mathfrak e_{6}\); hence \(-K\), and therefore
\(\mathfrak g=f^{2}(-K)\), is positive definite on \(\mathfrak m\). (This
is the same fact that makes the symmetric space
\(\M=\MCoset\) Riemannian; EIII is Hermitian symmetric, hence Riemannian, and
carries no invariant indefinite metric.)

By \autoref{lem:Rank4} the tetrad
\(e_\mu^{a}=\theta^{a}_{\alpha}\partial_\mu\phi^{\alpha}\) is
invertible for a non-degenerate configuration. Choosing a
\(\mathfrak g\)-orthonormal basis \(\{u_a\}_{a=0}^{3}\) of
\(\mathfrak p_{0}(x)\), so that \(\mathfrak g(u_a,u_b)=\delta_{ab}\), and
decomposing \(\partial_\mu\phi^{\alpha}=e_\mu^{a}u_a^{\alpha}\),
\[
  g_{\mu\nu}
  =\mathfrak g(u_a,u_b)\,e_\mu^{a}e_\nu^{b}
  =\delta_{ab}\,e_\mu^{a}e_\nu^{b},
\]
which is symmetric, non-degenerate (invertibility of \(e_\mu^{a}\)), and
positive definite. Smoothness in \(x\) follows from smoothness of \(\phi\) and
of the Maurer-Cartan form. Openness and density of the non-degenerate locus
hold because \(\det e_\mu^{a}\neq0\) is an open condition satisfied by
generic immersions. Thus \(g_{\mu\nu}\) is a Riemannian metric on
\(\mathbb R^{4}\), namely the first fundamental form of \(\phi\).

The signature is \((4,0)\), not \((1,3)\): a pull-back of a positive-definite
form is positive semidefinite for every \(\phi\), so no choice of configuration
can produce a Lorentzian induced metric.
\end{proof}

This appendix proves \autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}, \autoref{thm:inducedEH}, and
\autoref{thm:Mass28} from \autoref{sec:sigma}: the uniqueness of the invariant

\begin{proof}[Proof of \autoref{thm:uniqueMetric}]
Write the Cartan decomposition $\mathfrak e_{6}=\mathfrak h\oplus\mathfrak m$
with $\mathfrak h=\mathfrak{so}(10)\oplus\mathfrak u(1)$.  For $g\in \ESix$
define the adjoint action $\operatorname{Ad}_{g}(X)=gXg^{-1}$. The isotropy
module $\mathfrak m$ is real-irreducible of complex type
(\autoref{lem:Irrep}): its commutant is
$\mathbb C=\operatorname{span}\{\mathbf 1,J\}$, with $J$ the invariant complex
structure. An invariant symmetric bilinear form on $\mathfrak m$ has the shape
$b(X,Y)=-K(TX,Y)$ with $T$ in the commutant, $T=a\mathbf 1+bJ$. Since $J$ is
$K$-antisymmetric, the $J$-part of $b$ is the (antisymmetric) K\"ahler form;
symmetry of $b$ forces $b=0$ there, so $b$ is proportional to the restriction
of the Killing form,
\(
  \mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}=c\,K_{\alpha\beta}.
\)
On the compact real form of $\ESix$ the Killing form is negative definite, so
$K|_{\mathfrak m}<0$; positivity of the $\sigma$-model kinetic term therefore
fixes $c=-f^{2}<0$, i.e.\ $\mathfrak g_{\alpha\beta}=f^{2}(-K_{\alpha\beta})>0$,
completing the proof.
\end{proof}

Gauge fixing for the local \(H=\SOTenXUOne\) symmetry follows the

\section{Functional Renormalization-Group Details}

This appendix records the functional renormalization-group machinery behind
\autoref{sec:FRG}: the exact flow equation and the regulator, the truncation
and projection rules, and the derivations of the one- and two-loop
$\beta$-functions quoted in the main text.

We adopt the background-field functional RG \cite{Wetterich1993,Morris1994}.  
For the coarse-grained effective action $\Gamma_{k}$ the exact flow
equation reads
\[
  \partial_{k}\Gamma_{k}
  =\frac12\Tr\Bigl[
    \Bigl(\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}+R_{k}\Bigr)^{-1}
    \,\partial_{k}R_{k}\Bigr],
\]
where $\Gamma_{k}^{(2)}$ is the Hessian with respect to the collective
fields
$\Phi=(\phi^{\alpha},A_{\mu}^{i},\psi)$
and $R_{k}$ is an IR regulator that suppresses modes
with $p^{2}\lesssim k^{2}$.
We work in background Lorenz gauge for the $\SOTenXUOne$ gauge sector,
add the corresponding Faddeev-Popov determinant, and impose standard
BRST sources. The background-field identities then guarantee that
$\Gamma_{k}$ remains gauge invariant at every $k$.

All threshold functions entering the flow are evaluated with
\[
  R_{k}(p^{2})
  = Z_{k}\bigl(k^{2}-p^{2}\bigr)\,
    \theta\bigl(k^{2}-p^{2}\bigr),
\]
where $Z_{k}$ is the wave-function renormalization of the field in question.
Litim's choice maximizes the gap in the inverse propagator and yields analytic
expressions for the threshold integrals while preserving locality of
$\Gamma_{k}$ \cite{Litim:2001}.

Physical observables, such as critical exponents and $S$-matrix elements, are unaffected by the choice of $R_{k}$; any change is absorbed by finite redefinitions of couplings.  Explicit regulator-variation checks for the present truncation reproduce the standard $\sim1\%$ scheme uncertainty.

The truncation employed in \autoref{sec:FRG} keeps only the lowest-dimensional
operators that mix under the RG flow,
\begin{equation*}\label{eq:TruncAnsatz}
  \Gamma_{k}
  = \int d^{4}x\;
    \left[
        \frac{1}{2f^{2}_{k}}\,\partial_{\mu}\phi^{\alpha}\partial^{\mu}\phi_{\alpha}
      + \frac{1}{4g^{2}_{k}}\,F^{i}_{\mu\nu}F^{i\,\mu\nu}
      + \frac{y_{k}}{2}\,\phi^{\alpha}\bar\psi\psi
    \right]
  + \Gamma_{k}^{\text{grav}}[\eta_{\mu\nu}],
\end{equation*}
where \(\phi^{\alpha}\) denotes the $32$ coset scalars,
\(F^{i}_{\mu\nu}\) the \(\SOTenXUOne\) field strength and
\(\psi\) the chiral matter multiplet of \autoref{tab:FieldContent}.
Higher-derivative and multi-scalar operators enter only at subleading
order in the derivative expansion and do not affect the universal data
extracted here.
We write \(t=\log k\) and denote
\[
  \eta_{f}\equiv-\partial_{t}\log f^{2}_{k},
  \quad
  \eta_{g}\equiv-\partial_{t}\log g^{2}_{k},
  \quad
  \eta_{y}\equiv-\partial_{t}\log y^{2}_{k},
  \quad
  \eta_{M}\equiv-\partial_{t}\log M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}(k).
\]
Following \cite{Percacci2017}, we absorb canonical dimensions by
\begin{equation}\label{eq:DimlessDef}
  \hat g^{2}=k^{2}\frac{g^{2}_{k}}{f^{2}_{k}},\quad
  \hat y^{2}=y^{2}_{k},\quad
  \xi=\frac{k^{2}}{f^{2}_{k}},\quad
  \kappa=\frac{k^{2}}{M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}(k)}.
\end{equation}
Differentiation of \eqref{eq:DimlessDef} gives the projection rules
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ProjGauge}
  \begin{aligned}
  &\beta_{\hat g^{2}}
    =\bigl(2-\eta_{f}+\eta_{g}\bigr)\hat g^{2},&
  &\beta_{\hat y^{2}}
   = ( -\eta_{y})\,\hat y^{2},\\
  &\beta_{\xi}= (2-\eta_{f})\,\xi,&
  &\beta_{\kappa}=(2+\eta_{M})\,\kappa.
  \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Hence the task reduces to computing the four anomalous
dimensions \(\{\eta_{f},\,\eta_{g},\,\eta_{y},\,\eta_{M}\}\) from the trace on
the right of the Wetterich equation.

For a simple algebra $\mathfrak g$ with adjoint index
$C_{A}$, fundamental index $T_{R}$, $n_{F}$ Weyl fermions and $n_{S}$
real scalars the background-field flow reproduces the standard
minimal-subtraction result
\begin{equation}\label{eq:appOneLoop:b0}
  \beta_{g^{-2}}
  =
  \frac{b_{0}}{48\pi^{2}},\qquad
  b_{0}=\frac{11}{3}C_{A}-\frac43T_{R}n_{F}-\frac16T_{R}n_{S}.
\end{equation}
For the $\ESix$ matter content
\((C_{A},\,T_{R},\,n_{F},\,n_{S})=(12,\,1,\,16,\,32)\), we obtain
$b_{0}=52/3$. The $32$ coset scalars are (pseudo-)Goldstone modes of the
breaking $\ESix\to\SOTenXUOne$; at one loop their two-point function is
protected by the coset symmetry, hence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:appOneLoop:etaf}
  \eta_{f}=-\partial_{t}\log f^{2}_{k}=0.
\end{equation}
$28$ of the $32$ scalars acquire a common mass
\(m^{2}=f^{2}\mu^{2}\) (\autoref{thm:Mass28}). Their one-loop
contribution is the functional determinant
\(
  (1/2)\Tr\log\bigl[-D^{2}+m^{2}\bigr].
\)
Using the heat-kernel expansion
\[
  \Tr e^{-s(-D^{2})}
   =(4\pi s)^{-2}\sum_{n\ge0}s^{n}a_{2n}(\Delta),
\]
and the Seeley-DeWitt entries in \autoref{tab:SDWcoeffs}, the term
proportional to \(a_{2}\) induces
\begin{equation}\label{eq:appOneLoop:EH}
  \partial_{t}\Gamma_{k}\supset
    \frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\,
       f^{2}\,R\sqrt{-\eta}
  \quad \implies\quad
    \beta_{M_{\mathrm{Pl}}^{2}}
      =-\frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\,f^{2}.
\end{equation}
The minus sign follows from
\(
  \mu\,\partial_{\mu}\log(\Lambda^{2}/\mu^{2})=-2
\)
after identifying \(k=\mu\).
The Yukawa screening coefficient entering $\eta_{y}$ is
\(
  b_{y}=2C_{F}/(16\pi^{2})\,,
  \;
  C_{F}=9/4,
\)
so that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:appOneLoop:etay}
  \eta_{y}= 2\,b_{y}\,\hat y^{2}.
\end{equation}
Collecting \eqref{eq:appOneLoop:b0}--\eqref{eq:appOneLoop:etay}, one finds
\[
  \eta_{f}=0,\quad
  \eta_{g}= \frac{b_{0}}{24\pi^{2}}\hat g^{2},\quad
  \eta_{y}= 2b_{y}\hat y^{2},\quad
  \eta_{M}= +\frac{5}{48\pi^{2}}\kappa.
\]
Inserting these values into the projection identities
\eqref{eq:ProjGauge} gives the full canonical running. The main text quotes
the reduced loop component after subtracting the canonical \(2\hat g^{2}\)
term in the gauge direction.

Let
\(\bigl(\xi_{\ast},\hat g^{2}_{\ast},
        \hat y^{2}_{\ast},\kappa_{\ast}\bigr)\)
denote a simultaneous zero of the algebraic $\beta$-system, without assuming
that the zero lies in the positive-coupling domain. Linearizing
\(\partial_{t}\delta\mathbf g=M\,\delta\mathbf g\), one obtains
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Mmatrix}
  M=
  \begin{pmatrix}
    -2 & -A\,\xi_{\ast} & -b_{0}\,\xi_{\ast} & 0 \\
     0 &  2\hat g^{2}_{\ast} b_{1}
        &  2\hat g^{2}_{\ast} c_{1} & 0 \\
     0 &  c_{2}\hat y^{2}_{\ast}
        &  2b_{y}\hat y^{2}_{\ast} & 0 \\
     0 &  0 & 0
        & -2-\dfrac{5}{24\pi^{2}}\kappa_{\ast}
  \end{pmatrix}.
\end{equation}
For the coefficients of \autoref{tab:TwoLoopCoeffs}, the nontrivial
gauge--Yukawa branch has \(\hat g^{2}_{\ast}<0\) and
\(\hat y^{2}_{\ast}<0\). It is therefore excluded from the physical
positive-coupling regime. The stability matrix is retained here only as an
audit of the printed truncation; it is not evidence for an autonomous
positive-coupling asymptotic-safety fixed point.

Within the effective coset regime below \(\epsilon<1/6\), the one-loop
\(2\to2\) amplitude for canonically normalized coset scalars has the form
\[
  \mathcal A^{(1)}
  =\frac{\hat g^{4}}{16\pi^{2}}
    \log\frac{s}{t}
  +\text{cyclic}(s,t,u),
\]
which is finite inside the effective truncation. See \cite{Percacci2017} for
method details.

\section{Scalar-Stability Audit}
\label{app:Scalar27}
%======================================================================%
Earlier drafts organized an auxiliary \(27\times27\) quartic stability block in
the fundamental \(\mathbf{27}\) of \(\ESix\). We do not use that block in the
present argument. The physical coset fluctuations studied in this paper belong
to the \(32\)-real-dimensional tangent representation of EIII, with
complexification
\[
  \mathbf{16}_{-3}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{16}}_{+3},
\]
and their degeneracy is governed instead by \autoref{lem:Irrep} and
\autoref{thm:Mass28}. Consequently no independent conclusion in the paper
depends on a \(27\times27\) scalar stability matrix.
